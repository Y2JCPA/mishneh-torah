🎓 Quiz

הלכות סנהדרין פרק ט״ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
How tall is the platform from which the condemned is pushed in the stoning process?
The execution platform for stoning is two stories high — enough that a fall would typically be fatal. The push is from behind at the loins, so the condemned lands on his chest.
Question 2
In the burning method of execution, how is the burning actually accomplished?
Rambam describes burning as forcing the condemned's mouth open (by pulling a cloth around his neck) and then pouring melted tin or lead into his mouth — the burning is internal, not external.
Question 3
When must a hanged body be taken down, according to Jewish law?
The verse 'Do not let his corpse tarry overnight on the beam' mandates immediate removal — the body is hung near sunset and released immediately. Leaving it overnight violates a negative commandment.
Question 4
What is the total number of capital transgressions enumerated by Rambam across the four execution methods?
There are 18 stoning offenses + 10 burning offenses + 2 decapitation offenses + 6 strangulation offenses = 36 total capital transgressions in the Torah.
Question 5
Which two types of criminals are hung after their execution (post-mortem)?
Deuteronomy 21:23 refers to 'a person hung who is cursing God' — this is applied to the blasphemer. Numbers 15:30 equates idolatry with blaspheming God. Thus both blasphemers and idolaters are hung after stoning.
