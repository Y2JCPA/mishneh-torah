🎓 Quiz

הלכות סנהדרין פרק י״ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
Why do the Sages limit lashes to 39 rather than the Torah's stated maximum of 40?
By stopping at 39, any accidental extra blow still leaves the total at 40 — the maximum. This way the attendant can strike with full force on the final blow without risking violation of 'Do not add.'
Question 2
If the court estimates a person can bear 20 lashes, how many does he actually receive?
Estimates must be in multiples of three (to allow equal distribution). 20 is not divisible by three, so the next lower multiple of three is 18. He receives 18 lashes.
Question 3
What happens if a condemned person soils himself DURING lashing?
The verse 'your brother will be degraded' means once degraded through loss of bodily control during the lashing, he is exempt from further blows. The punishment's purpose has been fulfilled.
Question 4
What spiritual consequence is resolved when a person liable for karet receives their lashes?
Rambam rules explicitly: all those obligated for karet who receive their lashes are absolved from the heavenly excision. Earthly punishment atones and restores the person's status fully.
Question 5
After a rosh yeshiva (head of the academy) receives lashes for a transgression, what is his status?
Unlike a High Priest who returns to full status, a rosh yeshiva who sins is lashed but permanently demoted — not even to a regular Sanhedrin seat. This follows the principle that holiness levels may only ascend, never descend.
