הלכות מלכים ומלחמות פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which of the following is permitted regarding a deceased king's possessions?
When a king dies, his possessions are burned before his bier. Only another king may make use of his servants and attendants — which is why Avishag was permitted to Solomon but not to Adoniyahu.
Question 2
Why may a king not perform chalitzah?
The chalitzah ritual involves the woman spitting before the man — a degrading act. Since even a willing king cannot forgo his honor, he neither performs chalitzah nor yibbum.
Question 3
When must the king stand before the High Priest?
The king stands before the High Priest only when consulting the Urim and Tumim. Otherwise, though the High Priest honors the king, the king need not stand before the High Priest publicly.
Question 4
What does the Rambam say about the king's heart?
Citing Psalms 109:22, the Rambam says the king's heart must be 'a void within him.' Despite great external honor, internally he must be humble — modeled on Moses, the greatest of all.
Question 5
What is the relationship between a king's widow and future marriage?
A king's wife is permanently forbidden to others. Even another king may not marry a former king's widow or divorcee. This is part of the absolute dignity of the royal person.
