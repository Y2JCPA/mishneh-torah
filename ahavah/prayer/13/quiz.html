הלכות תפילה ונשיאת כפים פרק י״ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
Why do kohanim remove their shoes before Birkat Kohanim?
The principle of "remove your shoes — you stand on holy ground" applies. The Birkat Kohanim platform is sacred space.
Question 2
What happens if the chazzan forgets to call out "Kohanim!"?
The Rambam rules: kohanim should not duchan without being called. They wait for the chazzan's "Kohanim!" cue. The call is part of the procedure.
Question 3
When does the congregation answer "Amen" during Birkat Kohanim?
The congregation answers Amen after each of the three complete verses — not after individual words. Three "Amens" total during the blessing.
Question 4
Should the congregation look directly into the kohanim's raised hands?
The congregation faces the kohanim (to receive the blessing) but should not stare directly into their hands. Looking down is the practice.
Question 5
How do kohanim recite the blessing — reading it ahead, or prompted word by word?
The chazzan (or appointed person) calls out each word; the kohanim repeat it. This ensures the proper pace and connection with the congregation.
