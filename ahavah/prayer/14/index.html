הלכה א׳

The Sages extended the scope of the requirement and obligated the priests to bless the people in all the above services (Ta'anit 26b).The Ramah (Orach Chayim 128:44) writes:It is customary in these countries to recite the priestly blessing only on holidays, when people are in festive and joyous spirits...In contrast, on other days - even on Sabbaths - [the priests] are disturbed, worrying about earning a livelihood and the delay of work.Even on holidays, the priestly blessing is recited only in the Musaf service, directly before the people leave the synagogue and rejoice in the holiday festivities.The Ramah's decision is based on the view that the priestly blessings must be recited with feelings of joy and goodwill, and if those feelings cannot be aroused (see Zohar III 147a), it is proper that the blessing not be recited. Though the Ashkenazic community follows his view, in Egypt and in Eretz Yisrael the priests have always fulfilled the mitzvah of blessing the people every day. Many Ashkenazic authorities, among them Rav Shneur Zalman of Liadi and Rav Shlomo Kluger, desired to introduce this practice in the Ashkenazic community. After finishing the three verses, kohanim face toward the Ark again (away from congregation) while the chazzan completes the blessing in the repetition. 🧔 Accommodations
A kohen with a severe speech impediment may not duchan (can't say the words clearly). A kohen with blemished hands that would cause the congregation to stare at him — doesn't duchan (distraction). Multiple kohanim all say the verses together, not sequentially. If no kohanim present — the chazzan says the verses (as blessings, not Birkat Kohanim).

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 14
🔁
Finish What You Started
Even if a disqualification is discovered mid-blessing, complete it. Starting is a commitment.
🧔
Clarity is Required
The blessing must be audible and clear. A severe speech impediment prevents the blessing from reaching the congregation properly.
📊
Together Not Sequentially
Multiple kohanim bless simultaneously — a unified voice, not taking turns.
🙏
Congregation Must Pay Attention
The congregation faces the kohanim and listens. Their attention is part of receiving the blessing.
