הלכה א׳
מִצְוַת עֲשֵׂה עַל כָּל אִישׁ וְאִישׁ מִיִּשְׂרָאֵל לִכְתֹּב סֵפֶר תּוֹרָה לְעַצְמוֹ שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר (דברים לא יט) ״וְעַתָּה כִּתְבוּ לָכֶם אֶת הַשִּׁירָה״. כְּלוֹמַר כִּתְבוּ לָכֶם תּוֹרָה שֶׁיֵּשׁ בָּהּ שִׁירָה זוֹ, לְפִי שֶׁאֵין כּוֹתְבִין אֶת הַתּוֹרָה פָּרָשִׁיּוֹת פָּרָשִׁיּוֹת. וְאַף עַל פִּי שֶׁהִנִּיחוּ לוֹ אֲבוֹתָיו סֵפֶר תּוֹרָה מִצְוָה לִכְתֹּב מִשֶּׁלּוֹ. ואִם כְּתָבוֹ בְּיָדוֹ הֲרֵי הוּא כְּאִלּוּ קִבְּלָהּ מֵהַר סִינַי. וְאִם אֵינוֹ יוֹדֵעַ לִכְתֹּב אֲחֵרִים כּוֹתְבִין לוֹ. וְכָל הַמַּגִּיהַּ סֵפֶר תּוֹרָה וַאֲפִלּוּ אוֹת אַחַת הֲרֵי הוּא כְּאִלּוּ כְּתָבוֹ כֻּלּוֹ:

📖 Every Jew's Obligation
It's a positive commandment for every Jewish man to write a Torah scroll — or have one written for him. Even if you inherited one, you should write your own. A king must write two: one for the treasury and one that accompanies him everywhere. The scroll must be written in Assyrian script, with ink, on parchment, by a qualified scribe with intent. Corrections are permitted — but only by erasing and rewriting, not by pasting over.

כָּל כִּתְבֵי הַקֹּדֶשׁ אֵין כּוֹתְבִין אוֹתָן אֶלָּא בְּשִׂרְטוּט אֲפִלּוּ כְּתָבָן עַל הַנְּיָר. וּמֻתָּר לִכְתֹּב שָׁלֹשׁ תֵּבוֹת בְּלֹא שִׂרְטוּט. יוֹתֵר עַל זֶה אָסוּר. כֶּרֶךְ זֶה שֶׁיֵּשׁ בּוֹ תּוֹרָה נְבִיאִים וּכְתוּבִים אֵין קְדֻשָּׁתוֹ כִּקְדֻשַּׁת סֵפֶר תּוֹרָה אֶלָּא כְּחֻמָּשׁ מִן הַחֻמָּשִׁים. דִּין הַיָּתֵר כְּדִין הֶחָסֵר:
All sacred texts may be written only on a ruled [surface]. [This applies] even if they are written on paper. One may write three words without ruling [the surface on which they are written]. Writing any more than that is forbidden.A scroll that includes the Torah, the Prophets, and the Holy Scriptures does not possess the same degree of holiness as a Torah scroll. Rather, it is like a scroll containing one of the books of the Torah, because the addition [of a book in the scroll] is equivalent to having omitted one.
👑 Treating It Like Royalty
A Torah scroll is treated with extreme reverence: don't sit on a bench where one lies. Don't place it on the floor. Don't sell a Torah scroll (even to buy others) except in two cases: to fund Torah study or marriage. When reading, you may hold the wooden rollers but not touch the parchment directly. A worn-out Torah scroll: buried, not destroyed — like a deceased sage.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 7
📖
Write Your Own
Even inheriting a scroll doesn't fulfill the mitzvah. Writing one yourself is a personal covenant act.
👑
Two for the King
A king writes two: one for the treasury (national) and one always with him (personal). Torah is both communal and intimate.
🪦
Buried, Never Destroyed
A worn Torah scroll is buried with dignity — like a deceased sage. Sacred texts don't go in the trash.
🚫
Never Sold (Almost)
A Torah scroll may only be sold to fund Torah study or enable someone to marry. Nothing else justifies selling it.
