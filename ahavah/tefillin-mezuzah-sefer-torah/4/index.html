💪 Where They Go
Head tefillin: on the forehead, centered between the eyes, at the hairline — from ear to ear is the zone. Arm tefillin: on the left bicep (or right for a lefty), tilted toward the heart. The bayit faces outward. The straps wrap around the arm, hand, and middle finger. A left-handed person wears on the right arm — "your weaker hand" is the principle.

🌅 Daytime Only
Tefillin are worn during the day only — not at night, not on Shabbat, not on Yom Tov (these days are themselves "signs" and don't need an additional sign). Chol HaMoed: the Rambam rules tefillin are worn. Two blessings: "l'haniach tefillin" on the arm, "al mitzvat tefillin" on the head. 🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 4
💪
Heart and Mind
Arm facing heart, head between eyes — tefillin connect emotion (heart) and intellect (mind) to God's word.
🌅
Day Sign Only
Tefillin are a "sign" — Shabbat and Yom Tov are already "signs," so tefillin aren't worn on those days.
✋
Weaker Hand
Left-handed person = right arm. The principle is "your weaker hand" — the non-dominant one.
📏
Order Matters
Arm first, head second (follows the verse order). Remove in reverse — head first, then arm.
