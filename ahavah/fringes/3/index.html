It is very shameful for a Torah scholar to pray without being wrapped [in a tallit].4See Rosh HaShanah 17b, which relates that when God revealed the thirteen qualities of mercy to Moses, "He wrapped Himself [in a tallit] like a leader of prayer and taught him the order of prayer."

A person should always be careful regarding the mitzvah of tzitzit, because the Torah considered it equal to all the mitzvot and considered them all as dependent on it, as [implied by Numbers 15:39]: "And you shall see them and remember all the mitzvot of God."
👔 Four Corners = Obligated
Only garments with four or more corners require tzitzit. The garment must be large enough to cover a child who can walk independently. Material: wool and linen garments are Torah-obligated; other materials (cotton, silk) may be rabbinic. Women are exempt — it's a time-bound positive commandment (worn during the day only). Night garments: exempt. A borrowed garment: exempt for the first 30 days.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 3
👔
Four Corners Trigger the Obligation
No four corners, no tzitzit. A round poncho: exempt. A rectangular shawl: obligated.
🌅
Daytime Mitzvah
Tzitzit are a daytime obligation — night garments are exempt. This makes it time-bound.
👩
Women Exempt
As a time-bound positive commandment, women are exempt (though they may choose to wear them).
📏
Minimum Size
The garment must be large enough to cover a child who walks independently — roughly an adult poncho-size.
