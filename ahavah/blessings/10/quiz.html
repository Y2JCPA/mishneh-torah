הלכות ברכות פרק י · 5 Questions
Question 1
What blessing is said upon seeing 600,000 Jews gathered together?
Chacham harazim — "who knows the mysteries" — is said when seeing 600,000 Jews together. Each person's mind is different; seeing them all at once acknowledges the divine wisdom that knows each one.
Question 2
What is the blessing upon seeing a site where a miracle happened for the Jewish people?
She'asah nissim — upon seeing historical miracle sites (like the Red Sea, or where a Purim miracle occurred). Connecting physical geography to historical divine intervention.
Question 3
The morning blessings (Birkat Hashachar) were originally said:
Originally each blessing accompanied its specific action (opening eyes = blessing for sight, standing = blessing for legs, etc.). They were later grouped into a morning sequence for practical consistency.
Question 4
Must a blessing be said before studying Torah?
Birkat HaTorah — the blessing on Torah study — must precede any learning. It's said once in the morning and covers all Torah study throughout the day.
Question 5
What blessing is said upon seeing an unusually beautiful person?
Seeing unusual beauty — human or natural — triggers: "Blessed are You... who has such in His world." Beauty is recognized as a divine gift worth blessing.
