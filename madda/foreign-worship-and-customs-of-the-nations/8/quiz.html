עבודה זרה פרק ח · 5 Questions
Question 1
Can bowing to a mountain turn it into a forbidden idol?
The Rambam rules that anything not manipulated or made by human hands cannot become a forbidden idol. Mountains, natural springs, and growing trees retain their permitted status.
Question 2
How is a gentile's idol nullified?
Any physical diminishment nullifies it — smashing the face, cutting extremities, pouring out libation wine. Even a gentile child can perform the nullification.
Question 3
Can a Jew's idol be nullified?
This is absolute: a Jew's idol can never be nullified, even if a gentile performs all the required physical defacement. The sin is too severe for the object to be "rescued."
Question 4
If a tree had an idol placed beneath it and the idol is removed, is the tree forbidden?
The tree was a platform, not an object of worship. Once the idol is removed, the tree returns to its permitted status.
Question 5
When is an abandoned idol considered nullified?
Abandonment must be voluntary. An idol left behind during war might be reclaimed later. Only peacetime abandonment shows genuine, permanent rejection.
