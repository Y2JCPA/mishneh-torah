עבודה זרה פרק י״א · 5 Questions
Question 1
Is it permitted to avoid traveling because a black cat crossed your path?
The Rambam lists this as a classic example of forbidden soothsaying: "bread fell from my mouth," "a deer crossed my path" — these superstitions are explicitly prohibited.
Question 2
What is the Rambam's view on astrology?
The Rambam is unequivocal: astrology is "falsehood and lies." This is one of his most rationalist positions, going further than many other authorities.
Question 3
Is saying "this house brought me good luck" considered forbidden soothsaying?
The Rambam distinguishes: noting that things went well (observation) is permitted. But making future decisions based on signs and omens (superstition) is forbidden.
Question 4
What is the Rambam's famous concluding statement about all these practices?
The Rambam's famous rationalist declaration: all sorcery, divination, astrology, and consulting the dead are lies. The Torah forbids them not because they're powerful, but because people are foolishly attracted to them.
Question 5
When can a Jew dress in the style of non-Jews?
The Rambam permits a Jew in an important governmental position to dress like non-Jews if the difference would cause real embarrassment. Need, not preference, is the criterion.
