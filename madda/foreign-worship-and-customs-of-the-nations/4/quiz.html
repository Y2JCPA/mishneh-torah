🎓 Quiz

עבודה זרה פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many judges are needed to condemn a city as an ir hanidachat?
Only the Great Sanhedrin of 71 judges can issue this ruling. This incredibly high bar ensures the judgment is virtually impossible to execute unjustly.
Question 2
How many people must lead the city astray for the law to apply?
The Torah requires plural instigators ("men of wickedness emerged") leading the majority of the city. One person seducing another is a different category (mesit).
Question 3
What happens to the property of righteous people who didn't worship idols?
The righteous lose movable property found within the condemned city, but their other possessions are protected. The judgment is destructive but precise.
Question 4
What happens to Torah scrolls found in the city?
Sacred writings (Torah scrolls, second tithe, etc.) must be buried (interred) rather than burned. Even in the most extreme punishment, kedushah is respected.
Question 5
Can the condemned city ever be rebuilt?
The Torah states it shall remain "a ruin forever." The city may never be rebuilt. The permanence reflects the absolute seriousness of communal idolatry.
