עבודה זרה פרק ט · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many days before an idolater's holiday must commerce stop?
Three days before their holiday: no buying, selling, lending, borrowing, or gifts. The concern is that the idolater will thank their god for the transaction.
Question 2
Is it permitted to sell weapons to idolaters?
The Rambam extends the principle: just as aiding idolatry is forbidden, so is selling weapons or war materials to those who may use them for violence.
Question 3
Can you enter a decorated shop near an idol temple?
Adorned shops are considered part of the idol's celebration. Unadorned shops are permitted — the decoration signals participation in the worship.
Question 4
Can a Jew attend an idolater's wedding if kosher food is served?
The prohibition is about participating in the celebration, not about food. Even if he serves kosher food in kosher utensils, attending the feast is forbidden.
Question 5
What should be done with money from selling a house for idol worship?
Money from the sale of a house for idol worship must be cast into the sea. The profit is tainted and no benefit may be derived from it.
