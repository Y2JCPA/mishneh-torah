עבודה זרה פרק י״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
Where are the pe'ot (sidelocks) located?
The pe'ot are at the "corners" of the head: the area from the temples down to approximately the bottom of the ears. Only shaving (not cutting with scissors) is biblically forbidden.
Question 2
How many "corners" of the beard does the Torah prohibit shaving?
Five corners of the beard may not be shaved with a razor. However, trimming with scissors is permitted for all five corners. The distinction is razor vs. scissors.
Question 3
What is the source of the tattoo prohibition?
The Rambam connects the tattoo prohibition to idolatrous practice. Pagans permanently marked themselves for their gods. The prohibition preserves bodily integrity and separation from idol worship.
Question 4
Does the prohibition against mourning self-harm apply to non-death grief?
The Rambam specifies: gouging and gashing "for the dead" is this prohibition. Self-harm out of anger or frustration is forbidden but through different halachic sources.
Question 5
Are women obligated in the pe'ot (sidelock) prohibition?
Women are completely exempt: they don't violate the prohibition by shaving their own temple area, and a woman who shaves a man's pe'ot also doesn't receive lashes (though the man does if he allows it).
