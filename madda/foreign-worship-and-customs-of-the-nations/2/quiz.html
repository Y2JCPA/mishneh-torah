עבודה זרה פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
Is it permitted to study idolatrous practices out of academic curiosity?
The Rambam forbids not just worship but even mental engagement: don't study their texts, don't gaze at images, don't even wonder "how do they worship this?" Complete separation.
Question 2
How does the severity of idolatry compare to other sins?
The Rambam states idolatry is equivalent to all other commandments combined. Violating it is like violating the entire Torah.
Question 3
What happens to a Jew who accepts a false god as true without actually worshipping it?
Even mere acceptance — acknowledging a false god as legitimate without any act of worship — desecrates God's glorious, awesome Name.
Question 4
What must someone do who hears God's Name blasphemed?
Anyone who hears blasphemy of God's Name must tear their clothing, and that tear may never be sewn back together. The permanence reflects the gravity.
Question 5
How is testimony given in a blasphemy trial?
To avoid repeated blasphemy during the trial, witnesses use substitute names. Only when the court needs to render its final verdict does one witness whisper the exact words.
