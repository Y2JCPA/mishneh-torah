עבודה זרה פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the difference between an ov and a yid'oni?
The ov practitioner burns incense and hears a voice seemingly from the ground or their armpit. The yid'oni places a specific bird bone in their mouth and falls into a trance.
Question 2
What does Molech worship involve?
Molech worship specifically involves handing a child to priests who pass the child through fire. The Rambam notes it applies to all children — sons, daughters, legitimate, or otherwise.
Question 3
Why are matzeivot (gathering-monuments) forbidden even for worshipping God?
The patriarchs used matzeivot to worship God, but once idolaters adopted the practice, God "hated" these monuments. The form became permanently tainted.
Question 4
Is prostrating on paved stone forbidden everywhere?
Prostrating on decorated paved stone is forbidden outside the Temple. Inside the Temple, it's permitted. This distinction separates legitimate worship from idolatrous imitation.
Question 5
Why can't trees be planted near the altar?
Idolaters planted trees near their altars as "sacred groves." To maintain complete separation, the Torah forbids planting any tree in the Temple courtyard — even decorative ones.
