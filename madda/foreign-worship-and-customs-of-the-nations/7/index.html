🛁 The Bathhouse Exception
Prepared food and wine for idols are forbidden — but produce that was merely designated (not yet offered) is permitted. Found items on an idol's head are forbidden; at its feet — permitted (might just be left there). A bathhouse with an idol is permitted to use — "the idol came into your territory, not the reverse."

🎓 Key Principles

The pillars of Chapter 7
🔨
Active Destruction
In Israel, don't just avoid idols — actively seek and destroy them. It's a positive mitzvah.
🚫
Total Prohibition of Benefit
No food, shelter, shade, wood, or any benefit from an idol, its accessories, or its offerings.
🌳
Contamination Spreads
Asherah wood mixed into bread, woven into cloth — the prohibition spreads to everything it touches.
🛁
Practical Exceptions
The bathhouse rule shows the Rambam's realism: when the idol is incidental to the facility's purpose, use is permitted.
