עבודה זרה פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
How did idolatry begin according to the Rambam?
The Rambam traces idolatry to a seemingly reasonable mistake: since God made the stars, honoring them should honor God — like bowing to a king's ministers. Good logic, catastrophic conclusion.
Question 2
What happened over generations as idol worship spread?
The tragedy is gradual forgetting. Over time, the intermediaries (stars, images) became the entire focus, and the knowledge of God was almost completely lost.
Question 3
At what age did Avraham begin questioning idolatry?
The Rambam says Avraham began thinking deeply at age 40 — though some traditions say he was as young as 3. Either way, he arrived at monotheism through pure reasoning.
Question 4
How was Avraham received when he began teaching monotheism?
Avraham faced persecution for his beliefs. The people of Ur tried to kill him, forcing him to flee. He stood alone against the world.
Question 5
What is the Rambam's key insight about the origin of idolatry?
The Rambam's brilliant insight: idolatry began with seemingly sound reasoning. This makes it more dangerous — it shows how logical steps can lead to the worst possible conclusions.
