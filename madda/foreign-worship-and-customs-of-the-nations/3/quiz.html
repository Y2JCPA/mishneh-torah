עבודה זרה פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which four acts of worship make a person liable regardless of how the idol is normally served?
These four acts parallel the Temple service and are universally prohibited before any idol, even if the idol is normally served in a completely different way.
Question 2
If someone worships an idol in a derisive, mocking way — are they liable?
The Rambam states: even worship in a derisive manner makes one liable, as long as it's the accepted mode of worship for that idol.
Question 3
Is it permitted to worship an idol out of love for its beauty, without believing it's a god?
Even without genuine belief, worshipping out of love (finding it beautiful) or fear (of its supposed power) is forbidden by the Torah. The act matters, not just the belief.
Question 4
Why can't you bend down to pick up a splinter near an idol?
The prohibition is about appearances (mar'it ayin). Bending down near an idol looks like prostration, even if your real intent is to pick up a splinter.
Question 5
What's the rule about a signet ring with a human image?
A nuanced distinction: a raised image on a ring looks like wearing an idol — forbidden to wear but OK to seal with. A recessed image creates a raised impression when sealed — forbidden to seal but OK to wear.
