🔥 Always Liable
Four acts make a person liable regardless of how the idol is normally served: prostrating, burning incense, pouring libations, and slaughtering. These are the classic Temple service acts — performing them for any idol is always forbidden, even if the idol is normally served differently.

⚠️ Intent Doesn't Help
Pouring feces or urine before an idol? Liable — if that's its accepted worship. Serving an idol in derision? Still liable. Even worshipping out of love (finding it beautiful) or fear (of its supposed power) — without believing it's a god — is still biblically forbidden.

🖼️ Don't Even Look Like It
Don't bend down near an idol even to pick up a splinter — it appears like worship. Don't drink from idol-fountain statues. Making decorative images (even not for worship) is forbidden. A signet ring with a raised image: don't wear it (appears like idol). Recessed image: don't seal with it (creates a raised impression).

🎓 Key Principles

The pillars of Chapter 3
🔥
Four Universal Acts
Prostrating, incense, libations, slaughter — always forbidden before any idol, regardless of how it's normally served.
⚠️
No Loopholes
Derision, love, fear — no mental state excuses idol worship. The act itself is what's forbidden.
👁️
Appearances Matter
Even actions that merely look like idol worship are prohibited. Perception is part of the prohibition.
🖼️
No Decorative Idols
Images in human form are forbidden even as pure decoration. The form itself carries spiritual danger.
