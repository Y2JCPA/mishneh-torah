דעות פרק ו׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
What does the Rambam say about the influence of friends?
The Rambam states it's natural for character to be shaped by friends and environment. Therefore, one must actively choose to surround themselves with righteous and wise people.
Question 2
If someone wrongs you, what does the Torah require?
The Rambam says: don't hate in your heart. Instead, approach the person directly: "Why did you do X to me?" Silent resentment violates Torah; direct communication resolves it.
Question 3
How should rebuke be delivered?
The Rambam requires that rebuke be given privately, gently, and with the clear intention of helping the person. Public embarrassment is never acceptable.
