דעות פרק א׳ · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the "straight path" according to the Rambam?
The "straight path" is the middle point between two extremes — balanced, neither too much nor too little.
Question 2
How does the Rambam recommend fixing a bad character trait?
Like a doctor prescribing extreme treatment: lean to the opposite extreme temporarily, then gradually return to balance.
Question 3
What does "walking in God's ways" mean?
God is called Gracious — so be gracious. Merciful — be merciful. Holy — be holy. Walking in God's ways means adopting His character.
Question 4
How does lasting character change happen?
The Rambam says: repeat the desired behavior again and again until it becomes a permanent part of your personality. Practice, not willpower.
Question 5
Is every character trait governed by the "middle path" rule?
While the middle is generally ideal, Chapter 2 clarifies that anger and pride are special cases where even the middle is too much.
