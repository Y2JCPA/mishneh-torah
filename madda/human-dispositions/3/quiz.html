דעות פרק ג׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
What does the Rambam say about someone who withdraws from all worldly pleasures?
The Rambam explicitly rejects pure asceticism. He notes that the Torah requires a nazirite to bring a sin-offering — implying that unnecessarily denying yourself permitted pleasures is itself a form of sin.
Question 2
How can eating and sleeping become acts of serving God?
The Rambam teaches that when you eat to maintain your health so you can study Torah and do mitzvot, the eating itself becomes an act of divine service. Intention transforms the mundane into the sacred.
Question 3
What distinguishes a "chasid" (pious person) from a merely healthy person?
Both might follow the same health practices, but the chasid's motivation is different: everything is directed toward knowing God. This transforms ordinary self-care into constant worship.
