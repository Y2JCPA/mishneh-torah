דעות פרק ד׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
How full should you eat according to the Rambam?
The Rambam says to stop eating at about three-quarters of full satisfaction. Overeating is "like poison to the body" and is the root of most illness.
Question 2
What does the Rambam call exercise?
The Rambam states: "As long as one exercises, exerts himself greatly, does not eat to satiation, and has loose bowels, he will not become ill and his strength will increase." Exercise is fundamental, not optional.
Question 3
What must a Torah sage's city have?
The Rambam lists essential community infrastructure: a doctor, a bloodletter (medical practitioner), a bathhouse, a laundry, fresh water, a synagogue, a teacher, a scribe, a charity fund, and a court. Health services are Torah requirements.
