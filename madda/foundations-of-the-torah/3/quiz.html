יסודי התורה פרק ג׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
What does the Rambam mean by "Ma'aseh Merkavah" and "Ma'aseh Bereshit"?
The Rambam identifies Ma'aseh Merkavah (the Chariot) with metaphysics and Ma'aseh Bereshit (Creation) with physics. These represent the deepest level of Torah wisdom — what the Sages called "Pardes."
Question 2
According to the Rambam, what are the four elements that make up all physical matter?
Following Aristotelian physics, the Rambam describes four elements — fire, air (wind), water, and earth. Everything in the physical world below the moon is composed of these in various combinations.
Question 3
What makes the human intellect unique according to this chapter?
The Rambam teaches that the intellect is the human "form" — it's what defines us as human. Unlike the body, it's not physical and doesn't decay. It's what connects us to the realm of angels and to God.
