יסודי התורה פרק ה׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
For which three sins must a Jew choose death rather than transgress?
The three cardinal sins (yehareg v'al ya'avor) are: idolatry (avodah zarah), forbidden sexual relations (gilui arayot), and murder (shefichut damim). For all other sins, one should transgress rather than sacrifice one's life.
Question 2
What changes during a time of religious persecution (sh'at ha'shmad)?
During a decree specifically targeting Jewish practice, the rule changes: one must die rather than publicly violate even a minor custom (like changing one's shoelace style). The persecution makes every detail a stand for God's name.
Question 3
What constitutes "chillul Hashem" for a Torah scholar?
The Rambam says a person of great Torah knowledge who is dishonest in business, rude to people, or doesn't pay promptly creates a chillul Hashem — people see the behavior and it reflects badly on Torah.
