יסודי התורה פרק ה׳ · 5 Questions
Question 1
For which three sins must a Jew die rather than transgress?
Yehareg v'al ya'avor: idolatry (avodah zarah), forbidden relations (gilui arayot), and murder (shefichut damim).
Question 2
What changes during religious persecution?
During a decree targeting Judaism, even a minor custom becomes worth dying for — it's about standing firm.
Question 3
What is "chillul Hashem" for a Torah scholar?
A Torah scholar who behaves dishonestly or rudely creates a chillul Hashem — people judge Torah by its representatives.
Question 4
Is a person who sacrifices their life when halacha says to transgress considered righteous?
The Rambam states clearly: someone who dies when the halacha says to transgress is "responsible for their own death." The rules go both ways.
Question 5
Can prohibited substances be used for healing?
The Rambam permits using prohibited substances for healing only when life is in danger and only when the therapeutic benefit doesn't come through the normal way of eating.
