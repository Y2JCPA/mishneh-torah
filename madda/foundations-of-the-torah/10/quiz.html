יסודי התורה פרק י׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
How does a prophet prove that he is genuine?
The Rambam says a prophet proves himself by predicting future events that come true. If all his predictions are consistently accurate, he is accepted. If even one detail fails, he is false.
Question 2
If a prophet predicts punishment for a city and it doesn't happen, is he a false prophet?
The Rambam distinguishes: prophecies of doom can be reversed through repentance (as with Yonah and Nineveh). But a prophecy of GOOD that doesn't come true means the prophet is definitely false — God doesn't retract promised good.
Question 3
What is the key difference between a true prophet and a sorcerer/diviner?
The Rambam says the distinction is accuracy: sorcerers and diviners make predictions, and some come true while others don't. A true prophet's words are 100% reliable — "not one thing falls to the ground."
