📍 Where We Are

THE STRUCTURE
The Rambam opens his entire Mishneh Torah with the most basic question: Does God exist?

Before any halacha about Shabbat, kashrus, or prayer, he establishes the philosophical foundation everything rests on.
THIS CHAPTER COVERS
①God's existence is the foundation of all foundations (הלכות א-ד)
②Knowing God is a positive mitzvah (הלכות ה-ו)
③God is One — absolute unity (הלכה ז)
④God has no body or form (הלכות ח-י״ב)

💡 Key Principles

① יסוד היסודות
God's existence is the foundation of all foundations. Without it, nothing else in Torah has meaning. This is why the Rambam starts here.
② Necessary vs. Contingent
God is the only necessary being — everything else could theoretically not exist. God cannot not exist. This is what "true existence" means.
③ Absolute Unity
God's oneness isn't like "one apple." It's a unique, incomparable unity — no parts, no body, no division.
④ Torah Speaks in Human Language
Physical descriptions of God ("hand of God," "eyes of God") are metaphors for human understanding, not literal descriptions.
