🎓 Quiz

יסודי התורה פרק ט׳ · 5 Questions
Question 1
Can the Torah ever be changed or replaced?
The Torah is God's commandment forever. No change, no addition, no subtraction. Anyone who claims otherwise is false.
Question 2
Can a prophet issue a ruling that seems to contradict Torah?
Temporary emergency rulings are permitted (like Eliyahu on Carmel), but never permanent changes, and NEVER permitting idolatry.
Question 3
What happens to a prophet who claims to permanently add to Torah?
A prophet who permanently adds or subtracts is a false prophet — executed by strangulation, regardless of signs or wonders.
Question 4
What role do legitimate future prophets play?
Future prophets don't bring new Torah. They reinforce existing mitzvot, warn against sin, or address current events with temporary rulings.
Question 5
Is there any mitzvah for which even a temporary prophetic exception is impossible?
The temporary emergency exception does NOT apply to idolatry. No prophet can ever permit it, even for a moment. This is the absolute red line.
