יסודי התורה פרק ט׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
According to the Rambam, can the Torah ever be changed or replaced?
The Rambam states categorically: the Torah is God's commandment forever. No change, no addition, no subtraction. Anyone who claims otherwise is a false prophet.
Question 2
Can a prophet ever issue a ruling that seems to contradict the Torah?
A prophet can issue temporary emergency rulings that appear to violate Torah (the Rambam cites Eliyahu sacrificing on Mount Carmel). However, they can never claim it's a permanent change, and they can NEVER permit idolatry even temporarily.
Question 3
What happens to a prophet who claims God wants to permanently add a new mitzvah to the Torah?
The Rambam rules that any prophet who permanently adds to or subtracts from the Torah, or claims a mitzvah was only temporary, is a false prophet — and is executed by strangulation, regardless of any signs or wonders.
