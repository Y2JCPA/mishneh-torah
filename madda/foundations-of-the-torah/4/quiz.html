יסודי התורה פרק ד׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
How many names of God have full sanctity and may never be erased?
The Rambam lists 7 names that may never be erased: the Tetragrammaton (YHVH), Kel, Elokim, Kel-okim, Ekyeh, Shakkai, and Tzevakot. The Tetragrammaton is unique — it alone describes God's essence.
Question 2
What is the Rambam's position on how the world was created?
The Rambam affirms creation ex nihilo (yesh me'ayin) — God brought everything, including time itself, into existence from absolute nothing. Before creation, only God existed.
Question 3
What happens to titles like "The Great" or "The Merciful" when written about God?
The Rambam distinguishes between God's 7 sacred names and descriptive titles. Titles like "Great," "Mighty," "Awesome," "Faithful" are descriptions of God's attributes — they do not have the same sanctity and may be erased.
