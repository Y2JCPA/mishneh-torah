יסודי התורה פרק ז׳ · 3 Questions
Question 1
What are the prerequisites for receiving prophecy?
The Rambam says prophecy requires great wisdom (broad mind), strong character (refined middot), and never being overcome by the yetzer hara. Only someone who meets all these conditions is eligible.
Question 2
How was Moshe's prophecy different from all other prophets?
The Rambam lists 4 unique aspects of Moshe's prophecy: direct speech (no metaphor), while fully awake (not in a vision), without physical trembling or fear, and on his own initiative (not only when God chose).
Question 3
Can a prophet prophesy whenever they want?
Regular prophets must concentrate their attention and prepare themselves, and even after preparation, prophecy might not come. Moshe was unique in being able to receive prophecy whenever he wanted.
