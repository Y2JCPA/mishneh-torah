👨‍👦 The Chain of Transmission
A father must teach his son and grandson Torah. Women are exempt from this obligation (but receive reward if they study). If you can only afford one — your own study comes first, unless your son is sharper and more receptive.

📊 The Three-Part Split
Divide study into thirds: Written Torah (Tanach), Oral Torah (Mishnah), and Talmud (analysis and reasoning). As you mature, Talmud takes over — because understanding "why" is the deepest form of study.

🎓 Key Principles

The pillars of Chapter 1
👨‍👦
Generational Duty
Teach your son and your grandson. If choosing between your study and your son's, your own comes first — unless he's the sharper student.
📖
Universal Obligation
Rich or poor, young or old, healthy or sick — every man must study Torah. No exemptions for wealth or status.
⏰
No Retirement
The obligation to study Torah never ends. Until the day you die. There is no "graduation" from learning.
📊
Three-Part Study
Written Torah, Oral Torah, and Talmud. Over time, Talmud (analysis/reasoning) becomes the primary focus.
