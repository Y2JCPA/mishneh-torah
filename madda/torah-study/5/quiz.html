תלמוד תורה פרק ה׳ · 5 Questions
Question 1
Who deserves more honor — a father or a Torah teacher?
The Rambam rules: a teacher takes precedence. A father brings you into this world; a teacher brings you into the eternal World to Come.
Question 2
How close to your teacher can you issue independent halachic rulings?
Don't issue rulings within 12 mil of your teacher unless explicitly permitted. This protects the teacher's authority and prevents confusion.
Question 3
What should a student do if their teacher makes an error?
Never rebuke a teacher directly. Instead, pose the issue as a question referencing what the teacher previously taught — allowing them to self-correct with dignity.
Question 4
Can a teacher voluntarily give up the honor owed to them?
The Rambam allows a teacher to forgo their own honor. Unlike the honor of a king (which belongs to the office), a teacher's honor can be waived.
Question 5
What did the Sages say about students' effect on teachers?
"I learned much from my teachers, more from my colleagues, and most of all from my students." Teaching and being challenged by students deepens the teacher's own wisdom.
