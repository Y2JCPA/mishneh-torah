הלכות מעשה הקרבנות פרק ט״ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
If one vowed to bring a lamb for a burnt offering and brought a ram. What is the ruling?
A vow on a minor and brought a major — valid, because the major includes the minor within the vow.
Question 2
He said: 'An ox worth a shekel is upon me' and brought two oxen worth a shekel together. What is the ruling?
When the wording of the vow obligates a specific unit (one ox) at a defined value, it cannot be split into two.
Question 3
He said: 'One of the dedicated oxen' and he has several oxen. What is the basic halachic direction?
In matters of sanctity, generosity is assumed, and in cases of doubt, additional possibilities are considered to fulfill the obligation.
Question 4
One who says: 'It is upon me for the altar' without details — what does he bring?
The default is frankincense, as it is the entire nectar on the altar in its direct state.
Question 5
What is correct regarding libations in relation to maaser sheni in this chapter?
Even when there are limited allowances in maaser sheni for the body of the sacrifice or the bread of thanksgiving, libations remain an obligation from chulin only.
