The Foundational Commandment of the Incense Offering and the Sanctity of the Space

הלכות א׳–ג׳
הלכה א׳
מִצְוַת עֲשֵׂה לְהַקְטִיר הַקְּטֹרֶת עַל מִזְבַּח הַזָּהָב שֶׁבַּהֵיכָל פַּעֲמַיִם בְּכָל יוֹם בַּבֹּקֶר וּבֵין הָעַרְבַּיִם שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר (שמות ל ז) "וְהִקְטִיר עָלָיו אַהֲרֹן קְטֹרֶת סַמִּים". לֹא הִקְטִירוּ בַּבֹּקֶר יַקְטִירוּ בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם אֲפִלּוּ הָיוּ מְזִידִין. וְאֵין מְחַנְּכִין מִזְבַּח הַזָּהָב אֶלָּא בִּקְטֹרֶת שֶׁל בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם:
It is a positive commandment to offer the incense1See the description of the way in which the incense offering was prepared in Hilchot K'lei HaMikdash, ch. 2. offering2Sefer HaMitzvot (positive commandment 28) and Sefer HaChinuch (mitzvah 103) count this as one of the Torah's 613 mitzvot. Preparing the incense for the offering is not considered as a mitzvah (Sefer HaMitzvot, General Principle 10). Significantly, the Ramban (Hosafot to the negative commandments) maintains that the morning incense offering and the afternoon one should be considered as separate mitzvot. on the Golden Altar3See the description of the Golden Altar in Hilchot Beit HaBechirah 3:17. in the Sanctuary, twice each day, in the morning and in the afternoon, as [Exodus 30:7] states: "And Aaron shall burn on it the incense of fragrant spices."If one did not offer it in the morning, he should offer it in the afternoon even if [the omission] was intentional.4In his Commentary to the Mishnah (Menachot 4:4), the Rambam writes that it was rare that the priests willfully omitted offering the incense offering, because offering it brought blessings for prosperity. The Golden Altar may be dedicated only with the incense offering of the afternoon.5Note the contrast to the daily offering (Chapter 1, Halachah 12).
הלכה ב׳
כַּמָּה מַקְטִירִין מִמֶּנָּה בְּכָל יוֹם. מִשְׁקַל מֵאָה דִּינָרִין. חֲמִשִּׁים בַּבֹּקֶר וַחֲמִשִּׁים בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם. מִזְבֵּחַ שֶׁנֶּעֱקַר מַקְטִירִין הַקְּטֹרֶת בִּמְקוֹמוֹ. וּקְטֹרֶת שֶׁפָּקְעָה מֵעַל הַמִּזְבֵּחַ אֲפִלּוּ קְרָטִין שֶׁבָּהּ אֵין מַחֲזִירִין אוֹתָן:
How much [incense] was offered every day? [An amount that] weighed 100 dinarim,6A dinar is approximately 4 grams in modern measure. 50 in the morning and 50 in the afternoon.If the altar was removed from its place, the incense offering should, nevertheless, be offered there.7For the altar itself need not be there for the offering to be acceptable. Note the parallel in Hilchot Ma'aseh HaKorbonot 19:15. When incense - even a grain of it - flies off the altar, it should not be returned.8From the exegesis of Leviticus 6:3, Yoma 45b derives that the limbs of a sacrifice that fly off the altar should be returned to it, but not the grains of incense. The grain should be entombed (Radbaz).
הלכה ג׳
בְּעֵת שֶׁמַּקְטִירִין הַקְּטֹרֶת בַּהֵיכָל בְּכָל יוֹם פּוֹרְשִׁין כָּל הָעָם מִן הַהֵיכָל וּמִבֵּין הָאוּלָם וְלַמִּזְבֵּחַ לֹא יִהְיֶה שָׁם אָדָם עַד שֶׁיֵּצֵא זֶה שֶׁהִקְטִיר הַקְּטֹרֶת. וְכֵן בְּשָׁעָה שֶׁיִּכָּנֵס בְּדַם חַטָּאוֹת הַנַּעֲשׂוֹת בִּפְנִים פּוֹרְשִׁים הַכּל מִבֵּין הָאוּלָם וְלַמִּזְבֵּחַ עַד שֶׁיֵּצֵא שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר (ויקרא טז יז) "וְכָל אָדָם לֹא יִהְיֶה בְּאֹהֶל מוֹעֵד בְּבֹאוֹ לְכַפֵּר בַּקֹּדֶשׁ" וְגוֹ' בִּנְיַן אָב לְכָל כַּפָּרָה שֶׁבַּקֹּדֶשׁ שֶׁלֹּא יְהֵא שָׁם אָדָם:
When the incense is offered in the Sanctuary every day,9The Kessef Mishneh states that this phrase is making a distinction between the incense offering brought each day and that brought on Yom Kippur. Since the latter offering is brought into the Holy of Holies, it is necessary to depart only from the Temple Building. One may remain in the area between the Entrance Hall and the altar. See Hilchot Avodat Yom Kippurim 4:2. all of the people depart from the Sanctuary and from [the area] between the Entrance Hall and the altar. No one should be there until [the priest] who offers the incense offering departs. Similarly, at the time when [a priest] enters [to sprinkle] the blood of a sin-offering that is offered inside [the Temple],10This refers to the bull brought by a High Priest as a sin-offering, the bull brought as atonement for a law forgotten by the High Court, and the goats that are brought to atone for idolatry (see Hilchot Ma'aseh HaKorbanot 5:13). everyone departs from from [the area] between the Entrance Hall and the altar until he departs, as [Leviticus 16:17] states: "And no person should be in the Tent of Meeting when he enters to atone in the holy place." [This is] a general principle11This translates the Talmudic term binyan av. [applying] to all atonement [made] in the holy place: No [other] person should be there.
🔥 INCENSE TWICE DAILY
The incense offering is a daily fixed service in the morning and afternoon; at the time of its offering, absolute sacred exclusion applies within the Sanctuary and the area between the Entrance Hall and the altar.

The Practical Order of Offering the Incense

הלכות ד׳–ט׳
הלכה ד׳
כֵּיצַד סֵדֶר הַקְטָרַת הַקְּטֹרֶת בְּכָל יוֹם. מִי שֶׁזָּכָה בְּדִשּׁוּן הַמִּזְבֵּחַ הַפְּנִימִי נִכְנַס בִּכְלִי קֹדֶשׁ וְטֶנִי הָיָה שְׁמוֹ וְשֶׁל זָהָב הָיָה וּמַחֲזִיק קַבַּיִם וָחֵצִי. מַנִּיחַ הַטֶּנִי בָּאָרֶץ לְפָנָיו וְחוֹפֵן בְּיָדָיו הָאֵפֶר וְהַפֶּחָם שֶׁבְּתוֹךְ הַמִּזְבֵּחַ וְנוֹתֵן לְתוֹךְ הַטֶּנִי. וּבָאַחֲרוֹנָה מְכַבֵּד אֶת הַשְּׁאָר לְתוֹכוֹ וּמַנִּיחוֹ שָׁם בַּהֵיכָל וְיוֹצֵא. וּמִי שֶׁזָּכָה בַּקְּטֹרֶת נוֹטֵל כְּלִי מָלֵא קְטֹרֶת גָּדוּשׁ. וְכִסּוּי הָיָה לוֹ וּבָזָךְ הָיָה שְׁמוֹ וְנוֹתֵן הַבָּזָךְ בְּתוֹךְ כְּלִי אַחֵר וְכַף הָיָה שְׁמוֹ. וּמְכַסֶּה אֶת הַכַּף בְּבֶגֶד קָטָן וְאוֹחֵז הַכַּף בְּיָדוֹ וְנִכְנַס עִמּוֹ אֶחָד בַּמַּחְתָּה שֶׁל אֵשׁ בְּיָדוֹ:
What is the order of the offering of the incense offering each day? [The priest] who merited12I.e., who was chosen in the lottery, as described in Chapter 4, Halachah 6. to remove the ashes from the inner altar enters [the Sanctuary] [holding a] consecrated vessel called a teni.13The term is used in several contexts in the Talmudic literature to refer to a metal container. In his gloss to Tamid 3:6, Rav Ovadiah of Bartenura states that it has the same root as the word tene used by Deuteronomy 26:2 to refer to the container used to carry the first fruits. It was gold14As a sign of wealth and prosperity. See Hilchot Beit HaBechirah 1:19. and would contain two and a half kabbin.15A kab is 1376 cc according to Shiurei Torah and 2400 cc according to Chazon Ish. He would place the teni on the ground in front of him and scoop up the ashes and the coals on the altar with his hands16They were already burnt out and there was no danger of him being burnt. and deposit them in the teni. At the end, he would sweep off the remainder into it. He would leave [the teni] in the Sanctuary and depart.17Yoma 21a states this was done so because of the miracle that occurred. To explain: Our Sages relate the ashes from the Menorah and from the Golden Altar would be miraculously swallowed up in their place each day. In order not to require God to perform this miracle twice each day, the ashes from the Golden Altar would be put aside until those from the Menorah were collected. See Halachah 12.The one who merited to bring the incense offering18See Chapter 4, Halachah 7; Chapter 6, Halachah 4. would take a vessel filled to overflowing with incense. [The vessel] had a cover and was called a bezech.19In his translation of the Torah, Onkelos translates the word kaf, generally translated as "spoon," as bezech. Both the kaf and the bezech were utensils resembling ladles, i.e., they had a receptacle and a long arm leading to it. He would place the bezech in another vessel that was called a kaf. He would cover the kaf with a small cloth and hold the kaf in his hand. [Another priest]20The one who merited to remove the ashes from the altar, as stated in Chapter 4, Halachah 5. would enter [the Sanctuary] with him holding a fire-pan with fire.21I.e., with burning coals, as explained in the following halachah.
הלכה ה׳
וְכֵיצַד חוֹתֶה. זֶה שֶׁזָּכָה בְּמַחְתָּה לוֹקֵחַ מַחְתָּה שֶׁל כֶּסֶף וְעוֹלֶה לְרֹאשׁ הַמִּזְבֵּחַ וּמְפַנֶּה אֶת הַגֶּחָלִים אֵילָךְ וְאֵילָךְ. וְנוֹטֵל מִן הַגֶּחָלִים שֶׁנִּתְאַכְּלוּ בְּמַעֲרָכָה שְׁנִיָּה. וְיוֹרֵד וּמְעָרָן לְתוֹךְ מַחְתָּה שֶׁל זָהָב. אִם נִתְפַּזְּרוּ מִן הַגֶּחָלִים כְּמוֹ קַב אוֹ פָּחוֹת מְכַבְּדָן לָאַמָּה. וּבְשַׁבָּת כּוֹפֶה עֲלֵיהֶן הַפְּסַכְתֵּר. וְאִם נִתְפַּזֵּר יֶתֶר עַל קַב חוֹזֵר וְחוֹתֶה:
How does he collect the coals with the fire-pan? He takes a silver fire-pan22In his Commentary to the Mishnah (Tamid 5:5), the Rambam writes that this fire-pan would hold four kabbim. and ascends to the top of the altar and moves the coals to the side,23The Rambam's statements are based on his text of the Mishnah (Tamid 5:5). Apparently, the Ra'avad had a slightly different version. taking the coals that have been consumed by the fire in the second array24See Chapter 2, Halachot 4 and 8. and descends and places them into a golden fire-pan.25This fire-pan would hold three kabbim [the Rambam's Commentary to the Mishnah (loc. cit.)]. If a kab or less of the coals have become scattered [on the ground],26Since the golden fire-pan was smaller than the silver one, it was natural that this would occur (ibid.). he should sweep them into the channel [for the outflow].27As stated in Hilchot Beit HaBechirah 2:11, there was a channel for outflow that ran through the Temple Courtyard. Perhaps his question can be answered on the basis of Hilchot Tuma'at Ochalin 8:9 which states that these coals no longer have any holiness. On the Sabbath, he should cover them with the pisachter.28In his Commentary to the Mishnah (loc. cit.), the Rambam explains that in his Targum (Exodus 27:3 , Onkelos uses the term pisachter for the Hebrew sir, meaning "pot." If more than a kab become scattered, he should collect more with the fire-pan.
הלכה ו׳
שְׁלֹשָׁה דְּבָרִים הָיָה הַפְּסַכְתֵּר מְשַׁמֵּשׁ. כּוֹפִין אוֹתוֹ עַל הַגֶּחָלִים. וְעַל הַשֶּׁרֶץ בְּשַׁבָּת. וּמוֹרִידִין בּוֹ אֶת הַדֶּשֶׁן מֵעַל הַמִּזְבֵּחַ:
The pisachter would serve three functions: It was used to cover coals and a [dead] crawling animal on the Sabbath29See Hilchot Bi'at HaMikdash 3:20. and it was used to remove the ashes from the altar.30See Chapter 2, Halachah 13.
הלכה ז׳
וּמַקְדִּים לִפְנֵיהֶם זֶה שֶׁדִּשֵּׁן הַמִּזְבֵּחַ הַפְּנִימִי וְנוֹטֵל הַטֶּנִי שֶׁבּוֹ דִּשׁוּן הַמִּזְבֵּחַ וּמִשְׁתַּחֲוֶה וְיוֹצֵא. וְזֶה שֶׁבְּיָדוֹ הַמַּחְתָּה צוֹבֵר אֶת הַגֶּחָלִים עַל גַּבֵּי הַמִּזְבֵּחַ הַפְּנִימִי וּמְרַדְּדָן בְּשׁוּלֵי הַמַּחְתָּה וּמִשְׁתַּחֲוֶה וְיוֹצֵא. וְזֶה שֶׁבְּיָדוֹ הַכַּף נוֹטֵל אֶת הַבָּזָךְ מִתּוֹךְ הַכַּף וְנוֹתְנוֹ לְאוֹהֲבוֹ אוֹ לִקְרוֹבוֹ וְרוֹאֶה אִם נִתְפַּזֵּר מִן הַקְּטֹרֶת מְעַט בְּכַף אוֹהֲבוֹ אוֹ קְרוֹבוֹ נוֹתֵן לוֹ לְתוֹךְ חָפְנָיו זֶה שֶׁנִּתְפַּזֵּר עִם הַקְּטֹרֶת שֶׁבַּבָּזָךְ וּמִשְׁתַּחֲוֶה וְיוֹצֵא:
[The priest] who removed the ashes from the inner altar31Tamid 6:1 states that he would be accompanied by the priest who would clean the Menorah. The Rambam does not mention this point, because it is not germane to the discussion at hand. would proceed before them, he would take the teni that contained the ashes from the altar,32Which he had left previously in the Temple Sanctuary, as stated in Halachah 4. prostrate himself33As appropriate for one who completed his Temple service and depart. and depart. [He would then] prostrate himself and depart.The one holding the kaf would remove the bezech from the kaf and give it to a friend or relative.34I.e., thus four priests would enter: a) one to clean the altar; b) one to place the coal on it; c) one to pour incense into the hands of the priest who would offer it; and d) one to offer the incense on the coals (Radbaz). הלכה ח׳
וְאוֹמְרִין לָזֶה הַמַּקְטִיר הִזָּהֵר שֶׁלֹּא תַּתְחִיל מִלְּפָנֶיךָ שֶׁלֹּא תִּכָּוֶה. וּמַתְחִיל וּמַשְׁלִיךְ הַקְּטֹרֶת עַל הָאֵשׁ בְּנַחַת כְּמִי שֶׁמְּרַקֵּד סלֶת עַד שֶׁתִּתְרַדֵּד עַל כָּל הָאֵשׁ:
The one who offers the incense is warned:35As mentioned in Chapter 4, Halachah 7, the incense offering would be given to a priest who never offered it previously. Hence he would not be experienced and required these warnings (Radbaz). "Be careful. Do not begin [releasing the incense] in front of you, lest you become burned."36I.e., the person offering the incense stands to the east of the altar. He should begin pouring the incense on the western side of the altar. Otherwise, he will have to lean over the altar while the incense is burning and he could be burned in this manner. He begins to pour the incense on the fire gently, like one who sifts flour until it is spread over the entire fire.
הלכה ט׳
וְאֵין הַמַּקְטִיר מַקְטִיר עַד שֶׁהַמְמֻנֶּה אוֹמֵר לוֹ הַקְטֵר. וְאִם כֹּהֵן גָּדוֹל הוּא אוֹמֵר לוֹ הַמְמֻנֶּה אִישִׁי כֹּהֵן גָּדוֹל הַקְטֵר. וְאַחַר שֶׁאוֹמֵר יִפְרְשׁוּ כָּל הָעָם וְיַקְטִיר הַמַּקְטִיר וְיִשְׁתַּחֲוֶה וְיֵצֵא:
The priest who offers the incense does not offer it until the overseer37Rashi (Yoma 28a) maintains that this term refers to the segen, the High Priest's assistant. Tosafot maintains that it refers to the priest who apportions the Temple service by lottery. From Hilchot K'lei HaMikdash 4:16, it is obvious that the Rambam accepts Rashi's view. tells him: "Offer the incense." If the High Priest is the one offering the incense, the overseer says: "My sir, High Priest,38I.e., as expression of deference. offer the incense." After he says this, all of the people depart [from the area in front of the Temple Building].39As stated in Halachah 3. He then offers the incense, prostrates himself and departs.
⚱️ PRECISION IN SERVICE
From removing the ashes, through collecting the coals and transferring the dish, to the warning to the priest and the signal from the supervisor — every stage requires an exact order and reverence.

Cleaning and Kindling the Menorah

הלכה י׳

הלכה י״ב׳
מַהוּ דִּשׁוּן הַמְּנוֹרָה. כָּל נֵר שֶׁכָּבָה מֵסִיר הַפְּתִילָה וְכָל הַשֶּׁמֶן שֶׁבַּנֵּר וּמְקַנְּחוֹ וְנוֹתֵן בּוֹ פְּתִילָה אַחֶרֶת וְשֶׁמֶן אַחֵר בְּמִדָּה וְהוּא חֲצִי לוֹג. וְזֶה שֶׁהֵסִיר מַשְׁלִיכוֹ בִּמְקוֹם הַדֶּשֶׁן אֵצֶל הַמִּזְבֵּחַ עִם דִּשׁוּן הַמִּזְבֵּחַ הַפְּנִימִי וְהַחִיצוֹן וּמַדְלִיק נֵר שֶׁכָּבָה. וְהַדְלָקַת הַנֵּרוֹת הִיא הֲטָבָתָם. וְנֵר שֶׁמְּצָאוֹ שֶׁלֹּא כָּבָה מְתַקְּנוֹ:
What is meant by the expression "the removal of the ashes of the Menorah"?48Our translation is taken from the Rambam's Commentary to the Mishnah (Me'ilah 3:4) where the Rambam writes that deshen means "ash" and the remainder of the wicks and the coals produced are considered as ash. Every lamp that has burnt out should have its wick and its [remaining] oil removed and it should be cleaned. The priest should place another wick and other oil in it, using the half log measure. [The wick and oil he removed] should be placed in the ash-heap near the [outer] altar together with the ashes removed from the inner alter and the outer altar.49See Chapter 2, Halachah 12. He should kindle [any] lamp that was extinguished. Kindling the lamps is what is meant by the term hatavah.50Other authorities interpret hatavah as referring to the cleaning of the lamps. In his Commentary to the Mishnah (Tamid 3:9), the Rambam explains that this term means "the cleaning of the lamps, rekindling what was extinguished, and changing their wicks." See also Hilchot Bi'at HaMikdash 3:7 where it appears that the Rambam interprets this term as meaning cleaning and preparing the Menorah.As the Kessef Mishneh mentions, many other commentaries follow the approach of Rashba (Responsa 79 and 309) who maintains that the term hatavah means only the cleaning and preparing of the Menachot. This difference of opinion regarding the definition of this term is dependent on a larger issue. Were all the lamps kindled in the morning or not? As mentioned in Halachah 10, the Rambam maintains that they were. The Rashba and those who follow his approach differ. Accordingly, they differ in their interpretation of Exodus 30:7 "when he performs hatavah for the lamps, he shall offer the incense." According to the Rambam, this refers to the kindling of the lamps, while according to the Rashba, this refers only to cleaning them.Exodus 30:8 uses a different term for kindling the lamps at night (as the Rashba states in support of his approach). Perhaps hatavah can refer only to the rekindling of the lamps in the morning as indicated by the Rambam's Commentary to the Mishnah cited above. See the gloss of the Lechem Mishneh. When one discovers a lamp that has not been extinguished, he should restore it.51I.e., add oil so that it will burn until nightfall.

When any of the other lamps are extinguished, by contrast, they may be rekindled from each other.54Indeed, as stated in the following halachah, it is desirable to do so rather than use another candle.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 3
🔥
Fixed Services Are Not Easily Deferred
Regular Temple services such as the incense, the Menorah, and the chavitin supersede the Sabbath and ritual impurity within the framework of time-fixed sacrifice laws.
🚪
Absolute Exclusivity During Atonement Service
During the incense service or inner blood service, no other person may be present in the Sanctuary or in the area between the Entrance Hall and the altar.
🕯️
Tending the Menorah Is Active Maintenance
Hatavah is not merely cleaning but also replacing the wick and oil and rekindling any extinguished lamp, to maintain a continuous and dignified light.
⚖️
Continuity of Priesthood Requires Full Measures
In the chavitin offering, when a High Priest is replaced or disqualified, a complete isaron is brought and a legal determination is made between the portion offered and the portion burned.
