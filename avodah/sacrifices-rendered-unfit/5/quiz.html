הלכות פסולי המוקדשין פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the ruling if a person set aside money for a sin offering and died?
In Halacha 1, it is established that the sin offering money of a deceased owner goes to the sea, similar to a sin offering whose owner died.
Question 2
What to do if the original sin offering money was lost and new money was set aside?
In Halacha 2: As long as atonement has not been achieved, the funds are combined for one sin offering and the surplus goes to donation.
Question 3
What is the ruling if someone dedicated a sin offering thinking they were obligated, but later found they weren't?
Halacha 6 states that mistaken consecration does not take effect, thus the item returns to mundane status.
Question 4
Which leftover is said to 'decay' and not 'become a donation'?
In Halacha 8: Leftover flour/breads in certain cases (like thanksgiving and nazir breads) will decay, unlike libation money or sin offerings.
Question 5
What is the ruling if someone set aside money for an unspecified offering and died?
In Halacha 12: Unspecified funds are all suitable for a burnt offering, thus they go to donation.
