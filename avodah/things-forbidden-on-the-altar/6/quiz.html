🎓 Quiz

הלכות איסורי מזבח פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the ruling on wine, flour, or oil that became disqualified after being consecrated in a holy vessel?
In laws 4-5 it is established that after the consecration of a holy vessel, disqualification or impurity do not lead to regular redemption for these materials.
Question 2
In what case can the water libation on Sukkot return to being valid?
Law 6 distinguishes between purity before consecration (valid) and purity after the water has already been consecrated and disqualified (remains disqualified).
Question 3
Why are impure olives or grapes squeezed in less than a volume of a beitzah each time?
In law 7 it is explained that less than a beitzah of impure food does not render others impure, thus the liquids can remain valid for libations.
Question 4
Which of the following is disqualified for libations (and not just in the first instance)?
In law 9, boiled wine that has changed its taste is counted among the disqualified, while several other types are simply not preferred in the first instance.
Question 5
What is the exception to the rule that meal offerings and libations can come from abroad and from Mishnah?
Law 15 states that the omer and the two loaves must be from 'new' and from the land of Israel.
