הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
Who divided the priestly watches into their final number of twenty-four?
Moses originally established eight watches (four from Elazar, four from Itamar). Samuel and King David later divided them into the final twenty-four watches (Halachah 3).
Question 2
During a pilgrimage festival, which sacrifices may ONLY be offered by the designated watch of that week — not by all priests?
Vowed offerings, freewill offerings, and daily sacrifices belong exclusively to the watch serving that week even during the festivals, because these were established by the ancestors for each watch in its week (Halachah 5).
Question 3
If a priest is physically blemished and cannot perform the Temple service himself, what happens to his personal sacrifice?
Since he cannot appoint an agent (being unfit to serve), he gives the sacrifice to the serving watch who keeps the hide. However, since he is permitted to eat sacrificial meat, he receives a portion of the meat (Halachah 8).
Question 4
How is a High Priest initiated into office when the anointing oil is unavailable?
When there is no anointing oil, the High Priest is initiated solely by donning the eight garments of the High Priesthood for seven consecutive days, derived from the verse 'the priest from his sons who serves in his stead shall put them on for seven days' (Halachah 12–13).
Question 5
According to the Rambam's hierarchy of priestly precedence, which figure ranks ABOVE the segen (viceroy)?
The full order from top is: High Priest, then (in matters of service-readiness) installed High Priest, then war-anointed priest, then segen, then katikol, then amerkal, then treasurer, then watch head, then clan head, then ordinary priest. The war-priest ranks above the segen (Halachah 19).
