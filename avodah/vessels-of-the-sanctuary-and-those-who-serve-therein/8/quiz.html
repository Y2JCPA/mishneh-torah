🎓 Quiz

הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ח · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many garments does the High Priest's golden set contain, and what are the four unique to him (beyond the ordinary priest's four)?
The High Priest's golden garments number eight total: the four of an ordinary priest plus the cloak, ephod, breastplate, and forehead plate (Halachah 2).
Question 2
What happens to the High Priest's white garments used on Yom Kippur after the service?
The verse 'And he shall leave them there' (Leviticus 16:23) teaches that the Yom Kippur white garments are entombed on the spot and it is forbidden to derive any benefit from them (Halachah 5).
Question 3
A priest performs the Temple service wearing a tunic that is slightly longer than his measure, but he hoisted it up with his sash to the correct length. Is his service valid?
Halachah 4 rules that if a garment was worn-out or too long and the priest hoisted it with the sash to achieve the proper measure, his service is valid.
Question 4
What is the technical difference between a 'work of embroidery' and a 'work of craft' in Torah weaving terminology?
Halachah 15 defines these terms precisely: 'a work of embroidery' means the design appears on one side only, while 'a work of craft' means it appears on both sides of the fabric.
Question 5
Why were there four separate lockers for each priestly watch rather than one shared locker?
Halachah 9 explains that four lockers — one each for leggings, sash, hats, and tunics — prevented mixing so that priests could dress in the proper halachic sequence.
