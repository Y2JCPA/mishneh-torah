הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many permanent officers were appointed to oversee Temple functions, according to Chapter 7?
The Rambam rules that there were fifteen permanent officers in the Temple, corresponding to the fifteen positions listed in Tractate Shekalim — not a one-time arrangement but an enduring institutional structure.
Question 2
What was the minimum number of trumpet blasts sounded in the Temple on an ordinary weekday?
There were never fewer than twenty-one trumpet blasts daily: three at the opening of the gates, nine for the morning daily offering, and nine for the afternoon daily offering.
Question 3
What inscription appeared on the fourth type of Temple seal used for wine libations?
The fourth seal bore the word 'Sinner' (חוטא), referring to a wealthy person afflicted with tzara'at — a condition brought on by sin, particularly gossip — who brought a special three-animal sacrifice with accompanying libations.
Question 4
Why were new curtains made for the Temple every year?
According to Rabbenu Asher (Tamid 29b), the smoke from the daily incense offerings would discolor the curtains over the course of the year, necessitating the production of two new curtains annually.
Question 5
When a Temple curtain became ritually impure through contact with a *derivative* of impurity (not a primary source), where was it immersed?
A curtain impure from a derivative of impurity was immersed within the Temple Courtyard in the Sea of Solomon, which received water from underground springs. Since this was only Rabbinic impurity, no sunset-waiting was required and it could be returned to use immediately.
