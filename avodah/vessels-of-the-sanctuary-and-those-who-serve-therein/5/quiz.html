הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
If a man appointed as High Priest lacks personal wealth, what is the required response?
H1 states that if the High Priest lacks wealth of his own, all his priestly brethren should give him according to their financial capacity until he is wealthier than all of them.
Question 2
What is the High Priest forbidden to do regarding his hair, even during a mourning period when others are obligated to let their hair grow?
H6 rules that the High Priest may never let his hair grow long — not even when mourning — because Leviticus 21:10 states 'He shall not allow the hair of his head to grow long.' He must cut his hair every Friday with scissors.
Question 3
In what court are capital cases involving a High Priest adjudicated?
H8 states that capital cases against the High Priest are judged in the High Court alone, derived from Exodus 18:22: 'Any great matter will be brought to you,' implying matters of great importance — such as the life of the High Priest — require the full Sanhedrin.
Question 4
Which of the following correctly describes the High Priest's privileges regarding Temple sacrifices?
H12 states the High Priest may offer the incense offering on any day he desires, receives the first portion of any sacrifice offered in the Temple, and does not participate in the lottery — he may offer any sacrifice he wishes at will.
Question 5
A priest who has never served before is now appointed as High Priest. How many inaugural meal offerings of a tenth of an ephah must he bring?
H17 specifies that such a priest must bring three offerings: the first as the training of every ordinary priest, the second as the training of a High Priest, and the third as the chavitin offering that the High Priest brings every day — all offered in the same manner.
