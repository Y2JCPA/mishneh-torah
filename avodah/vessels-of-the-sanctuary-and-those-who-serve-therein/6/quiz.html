הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
Why did the men of the ma'amad NOT fast on Sunday or Friday?
Fasting on Sunday would force an immediate transition from Sabbath pleasure to deprivation, weakening them severely. Friday was excluded so they would have energy for Sabbath preparations.
Question 2
Which Torah portion did the men of the ma'amad read, and why?
They read the narrative of Creation because the Talmud teaches that were it not for the sacrifices, heaven and earth would not be maintained — the world's purpose culminates in the Temple service.
Question 3
How many prayer services did the men of the ma'amad recite each day, and which was unique to them?
The men of the ma'amad recited four services: morning, afternoon, neilah, and an additional service between morning and afternoon that was unique to them, during which they prayed for the sacrifices to be accepted.
Question 4
On a day when a Musaf sacrifice was offered (e.g., Rosh Chodesh), which ma'amad services were suspended?
On days with a Musaf sacrifice, the men were occupied with that additional offering, so the second and afternoon ma'amad services were suspended. Only the morning and neilah services continued.
Question 5
What was the 'sacrifice of wood,' and what restrictions applied to the families who brought it?
Specific families had fixed times to bring wood for the altar. On their day they also brought voluntary burnt offerings, making it a quasi-festival: eulogies, fasting, and work were all forbidden, even for a private individual who simply donated wood.
