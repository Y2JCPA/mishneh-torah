לרפואת פייגא בת יטא רבקה

🎓 Quiz

הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ט · 5 Questions
Question 1
What text was inscribed on the forehead plate (tzitz), and in what arrangement?
The standard was two lines with לה׳ (God's name) on top and קדש below, though writing both on one line was also valid — attested by a plate seen in Rome.
Question 2
How were the letters on the forehead plate made to project outward?
The craftsman engraved letters on the back of the plate while it was pressed to beeswax, which supported the gold without piercing it, causing the letters to project forward.
Question 3
How many total threads were used in weaving the gold-interwoven sections of the ephod and breastplate?
Each of the four materials (sky-blue, red, scarlet wool, and linen) was combined with one gold strand into a seven-thread group, yielding 4 × 7 = 28 strands total.
Question 4
What was placed between the two folded halves of the breastplate?
The breastplate was woven at cubit length, then folded in half to form a square zeret × zeret, with the Urim and Tumim placed within the fold.
Question 5
What is the halachic consequence of deliberately separating the breastplate from the ephod?
Separating the breastplate from the ephod with destructive intent violates a Torah negative commandment (Sefer HaMitzvot #87) and is punishable by lashes.
