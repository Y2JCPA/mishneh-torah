הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the minimum number of Levites required to stand on the duchan each day to sing?
Halachah 3 states there should never be fewer than twelve Levites on the duchan each day, corresponding to the twelve musical instruments playing, though their number could be increased without end.
Question 2
Which instrument has no upper limit on how many could be used in the Temple?
Halachah 4 specifies that there should not be fewer than nine harps, with no upper limit stated. By contrast, lyres were capped at six, flutes at twelve, and trumpets at one hundred and twenty.
Question 3
According to the Rambam, what resolves the apparent contradiction between the ages of 25 and 30 for Levitical service in the Torah?
Halachah 7 explains that Levites began studying at age 25 and entered active Temple service only at age 30, after five full years of preparation — resolving the two different ages cited in Numbers.
Question 4
In future generations (after the desert era), what is the ONE thing that disqualifies a Levite from Temple service?
Halachah 8 states that for future generations, Levites are not disqualified by age or physical blemishes (unlike priests), but only if their voice deteriorates — and even then, the Rambam suggests they could serve as doorkeepers rather than singers.
Question 5
If a Levite performs the service designated for a priest, what is the halachic consequence?
Halachah 11 states that when Levites perform priestly service (or one Levite assists in another's task), they are liable for death at the hand of Heaven, as derived from Numbers 18:3. This is stricter than the rule for priests who perform Levitical service, where only a negative commandment is violated.
