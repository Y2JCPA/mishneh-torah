הלכות כלי המקדש פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the ruling for someone who prepared anointing oil in the manner of Moses intentionally?
Rambam (Halacha 4) rules that one who makes oil in this manner intentionally is liable for karet, and unintentionally brings a fixed sin offering.
Question 2
How was the High Priest anointed with the anointing oil?
Rambam (Halacha 9) states that they pour oil on his head and anoint between his eyes in the shape of the Greek letter 'Chi'. The king, however, is anointed like a crown on his forehead.
Question 3
How was the High Priest consecrated during the Second Temple period without anointing oil?
Rambam (Halacha 8) explains that when the anointing oil was hidden, the High Priest was appointed to his position by wearing only the eight garments of the High Priest.
Question 4
Where should a king be anointed?
Rambam (Halacha 11) rules that a king is only anointed by a spring, as a sign that his kingship will continue, just as Solomon was anointed at the Gihon stream.
Question 5
What is unique about the measurement of the hin in the Temple, according to Rambam?
Rambam (Halacha 18) explicitly notes that nothing is measured in hin in the Temple — except that it was preserved from the days of Moses when he measured the oil for the anointing oil.
