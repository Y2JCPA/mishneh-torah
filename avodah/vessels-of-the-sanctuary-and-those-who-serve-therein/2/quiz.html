הלכות כלי המקדש פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many spices were included in the incense offering?
The incense contained eleven spices: four explicitly named in the Torah (balsam, onycha, storax, frankincense) plus seven communicated to Moses at Sinai, as stated in Halachah 2.
Question 2
What was the total weight of the annual incense mixture prepared each year?
The total mixture weighed 368 maneh. 365 maneh were burned daily (one per day of the solar year), and the remaining 3 maneh were re-ground finely for the High Priest's Yom Kippur service in the Holy of Holies.
Question 3
If a person prepares the exact incense formula privately in order to smell its fragrance, what is the penalty?
Halachah 9 rules that willful private preparation of the incense formula to smell it carries karet — even for a partial batch, as long as the proportions are correct. As Exodus 30:38 states: 'Anyone who prepares like this to smell its fragrance shall be cut off from his people.'
Question 4
What disqualifies the incense offering according to Halachah 8?
Adding any honey to the incense disqualifies it entirely. There is an explicit Biblical prohibition against burning honey as a Temple offering (Leviticus 2:11). Omitting any of the eleven spices also incurs death.
Question 5
How must the Ark of the Covenant be transported, and what is the source for this rule?
It is a positive mitzvah for the Ark to be carried on poles on human shoulders. David's violation of this by using a wagon led to Uzzah's death. Numbers 7:9 states: 'For the holy task is their obligation. They shall carry it on their shoulders.'
