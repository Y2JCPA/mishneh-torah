🎓 Quiz

הלכות שחיטה פרק י״ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which animals require blood-covering after slaughter?
The Torah specifies wild beasts and fowl. Domesticated animals and consecrated animals do not require blood-covering.
Question 2
If someone covers blood and it becomes uncovered again, what is the rule?
Once a person has covered the blood, the mitzvah has been fulfilled. No second covering is required.
Question 3
Which material is INVALID for covering blood?
Bran and flour are not classified as עָפָר. Gold dust is valid because Scripture calls it עָפָר.
Question 4
If the slaughterer fails to cover the blood, who is obligated to do so?
Anyone who observes the uncovered blood is obligated to cover it.
Question 5
Why must one not cover blood with the foot?
Treating a mitzvah with disdain reflects dishonor toward God, who commanded the mitzvot.
