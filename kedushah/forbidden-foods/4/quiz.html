🎓 Quiz

הלכות מאכלות אסורות פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
Minimum amount of nevelah for lash liability?
Liability is incurred by eating an olive-sized portion of nevelah.
Question 2
Why is a non-kosher animal not subject to the nevelah prohibition?
Nevelah applies only to kosher species since it describes an animal that could have been made permissible through slaughter.
Question 3
Which correctly defines a trefe?
A trefe is a kosher animal that received a fatal wound in specific recognized organs.
Question 4
A sick animal is slaughtered at night with no one to observe movements. Ruling?
If convulsive movements cannot be confirmed, the meat is forbidden as a safeguard.
Question 5
Can nevelah and non-kosher animal meat combine for the olive-size minimum?
They derive from different Torah verses and represent distinct categories that do not combine.
