הלכות גזילה ואבידה פרק י״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
A finder discovers his own lost article and a colleague's simultaneously, but can only retrieve one. Which takes priority?
Halacha 1: When a person discovers both his own and a colleague's lost articles, his own takes priority.
Question 2
Between a teacher's lost article and a father's lost article, which takes priority?
Halacha 2: A teacher's lost article takes priority over a father's, since the teacher grants one eternal life (Torah), while the father provides only physical existence.
Question 3
A caravan is attacked by bandits and pays a ransom to be released. How is the ransom divided?
Halacha 10: When a caravan pays a ransom, the calculation is made proportionally based on the value of each member's goods.
Question 4
When cargo is jettisoned at sea to save the ship, how is the loss distributed?
Halacha 13: When a ship jettisons cargo, the calculation is made according to the weight of each person's cargo — not its monetary value.
Question 5
If a person ignores his own loss to return another's property, how is he compensated for his personal loss?
Halacha 2: If a person ignores his own lost article to return another's, he is entitled only to the wage of a hired laborer for his time — not the value of his own loss.
