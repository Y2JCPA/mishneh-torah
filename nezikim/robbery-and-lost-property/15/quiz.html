הלכות גזילה ואבידה פרק ט״ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
A finder sees a wallet apparently placed down intentionally in a private domain. What should he do?
Halacha 1: An item that appears to have been intentionally placed should not be touched, whether in public or private domain.
Question 2
When is an animal seen pasturing in its normal place considered 'lost' and requiring return?
Halacha 2: An animal pasturing in an ordinary place during the day is not considered lost; at night, it is considered lost and must be returned.
Question 3
A finder discovers dyed wool left in a public area. Must he announce it?
Halacha 8: Dyed wool (a craftsman's product) must be announced — professional goods indicate an owner who expects to recover them.
Question 4
A finder finds berries set out to dry by the road. May he keep them?
Halacha 14: Berries set out to dry on the road may be taken — owners are presumed to forgo ownership over them.
Question 5
A perverse cat that kills young children is found. Must it be returned to its owner?
Halacha 16: A cat that kills young children may not be kept by its owner; taking it is not considered robbery, and one is not obligated to return it.
