הלכות נזקי ממון פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
An owner locks sheep in a corral with a gate that can withstand ordinary wind. The sheep escape and cause damage. Who is liable?
Halachah 1: If the owner guarded adequately (gate that withstands ordinary wind) and the animals escaped, the owner is exempt — he fulfilled his duty.
Question 2
Someone breaks down a sturdy fence and the animal inside escapes and causes damage. Who pays?
Halachah 2: When a third party breaks down a sturdy fence and the animal escapes, the fence-breaker is liable, not the animal owner.
Question 3
A person borrows an ox believing it to be tam. It turns out to be mu'ad and causes damage. Who pays?
Halachah 7: The owner/lender pays because he knew the ox was mu'ad and did not disclose it — his deception makes him liable.
Question 4
How is damage to growing crops assessed?
Halachah 12: Growing crop damage is assessed by comparison — the damaged area is evaluated relative to a surrounding field sixty times its size.
Question 5
A watchman entrusts an animal to a second watchman, and the animal causes damage. Who is liable to the injured party?
Halachah 10: The first watchman remains liable to the injured party — a watchman cannot transfer his responsibility by sub-entrusting to another.
