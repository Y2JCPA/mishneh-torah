הלכות גירושין פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
Who is responsible for paying the scribe's fee for writing the get?
Halacha 4 states explicitly: 'It is the woman who always pays the scribe's fee.'
Question 2
What is the halachic distinction between a get described as 'void' (bateil) versus 'unacceptable' (pasul)?
Halacha 7 explains that 'void' (bateil) means the get is invalid according to Scriptural law, while 'unacceptable' (pasul) refers to a get deemed invalid by Rabbinic decree.
Question 3
If a husband says 'Write a get for my wife' without instructing that it be given to her, what must the recipients do?
Halacha 12 rules that when a husband says 'Write a get for my wife,' the people addressed should write it, sign it, and give it to him — not to the woman.
Question 4
If a man gives valid divorce instructions while healthy, then later loses his mental faculties, what happens when he recovers?
Halacha 15 rules that once he recovers the get may be written and delivered — there is no need to consult him again.
Question 5
What is the status of a get issued under court compulsion to a husband required by law to divorce his wife?
Halacha 20 explains that a compelled get is fully valid because we assume that deep within every Jew is the will to comply with Jewish law.
