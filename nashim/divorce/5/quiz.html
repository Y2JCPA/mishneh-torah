הלכות גירושין פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
According to Halacha 1, which of the following locations can validly receive a get on behalf of the woman?
Halacha 1 states that whether the get is placed in her hand, bosom, courtyard, or given to her appointed agent, the same law applies.
Question 2
Why is a get thrown into a woman's courtyard NOT effective if she is absent from it?
Halacha 2 explains that divorce is considered a liability for the woman, and a liability may not be invoked against a person outside her presence.
Question 3
When does a get thrown upward toward a woman's roof take effect?
Halacha 3 rules that once the get reaches within three handbreadths of the roof, the divorce is effective — but only provided the get ultimately comes to rest there undestroyed.
Question 4
In the public domain, when is a get thrown to a woman considered definitively 'close to him' rather than close to her?
Halacha 14 defines 'close to him' as a situation where he can protect the get but she cannot.
Question 5
How does delivering a get via the husband's own servant become effective for the woman?
Halacha 18 rules that if the husband places the get in his servant's hand and then gifts the servant to the woman, when she acquires the servant she simultaneously acquires the get — provided the servant is bound.
