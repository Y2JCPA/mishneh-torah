הלכות אישות פרק כ״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
When does a husband's right to inherit his wife's estate first take effect?
Halacha 1 states that the husband acquires the right to inherit when his wife leaves her father's domain, even though she has not yet entered the chuppah, since she has entered his domain.
Question 2
If a woman sells property between her consecration and the consummation of the marriage, is the sale binding?
Halacha 8 states that if a woman sells property between consecration and consummation, the sale is binding, because the husband has no right to his wife's property until the marriage is consummated.
Question 3
May a yavam sell property from his deceased brother's estate before performing yibbum?
Halacha 11 states that a yavam may not sell any of his brother's property either before or after yibbum, and if he does, his actions are of no consequence.
Question 4
If a wife formally sells her nichsei tzon barzel to her husband with witnesses present, can she later reclaim the property?
Halacha 18 states that even when the husband formalized the transaction through a voluntary act of contract, the wife may recant whenever she desires, since we presume she acted only to maintain peace in her home.
Question 5
When a married woman inherits produce still attached to the ground, to whom does it belong?
Halacha 24 states that when a married woman inherits produce still attached to the land, it becomes the husband's property.
