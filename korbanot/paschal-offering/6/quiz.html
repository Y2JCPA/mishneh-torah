הלכות קרבן פסח פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which impure individuals must wait until the second Pesach?
Zavim, zavot, niddot, and others cannot partake in the Paschal sacrifice on the fifteenth of Nisan due to their impurity (Halacha 1).
Question 2
If the seventh day of purification falls on the 14th of Nisan, when can a person partake in the Paschal sacrifice?
The person can partake in the evening after completing the purification process. The sacrifice should not be slaughtered for them before (Halacha 2).
Question 3
What is the status of an uncircumcised Jew who circumcises himself the day before Pesach?
An uncircumcised Jew who circumcises himself just before Pesach can have the sacrifice slaughtered on his behalf (Halacha 7).
Question 4
Why is a person fit to partake of the Paschal sacrifice even during aninut at night?
The restrictions of aninut at night are Rabbinic and do not prevent partaking if the sacrifice was slaughtered in the day (Halacha 9).
Question 5
What happens if a person discovers they were impure due to 'impurity likened to the depths' after the Paschal offering?
If it was 'impurity likened to the depths,' they are exempt from the second Paschal sacrifice because the impurity was not known (Halachot 12 and 13).
