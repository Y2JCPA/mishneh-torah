הלכות שגגות פרק י״ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
When the court erroneously eliminates a fundamental law, who is liable for a sin-offering?
According to halacha 1, each transgressor is obligated, not the court, when a fundamental law is nullified erroneously.
Question 2
What type of ruling makes the court liable for a sin-offering?
Halacha 2 explains that the court is liable when their ruling errors involve specific prohibitions, not fundamental laws.
Question 3
If the court mistakenly judged that Sabbath ended due to cloud cover, what is required?
Halacha 3 states that individuals who acted on the mistaken judgement have to bring sin-offerings.
Question 4
What happens if a court forgets the specific nature of a prohibition they ruled on?
Per halacha 4, those who acted on the ruling must bring a sin-offering when specifics are forgotten by the court.
Question 5
Who is obligated to bring a provisional guilt-offering after a court becomes aware of their error?
Halacha 5 specifies that individuals who don't stay informed after a court corrects its ruling must bring a provisional guilt-offering.
