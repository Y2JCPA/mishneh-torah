הלכות שגגות פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is required to be liable for a sin-offering?
Halacha 1 states liability requires inadvertence from start to finish.
Question 2
Is one liable if they purposely lifted an object but inadvertently set it down on the Sabbath?
Halacha 1 exempts when part of the act, such as setting down, is inadvertent.
Question 3
Does awareness of karet change exemption status for unaware transgressors?
Halacha 2 specifies that knowing about karet without knowing the sacrifice obligation still makes it willful.
Question 4
What happens if one transgresses while performing a mitzvah strikingly similar to the sin?
Halacha 8 states inadvertency while performing a mitzvah negates liability, as seen in the example of circumcision.
Question 5
In which situation does a person remain liable for a sin-offering?
Halacha 16 shows liability when one eats leftover meat, failing a mitzvah despite good intent.
