הלכות בכורות פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
What animals are subject to the mitzvah of tithing as described in halacha 1?
Halacha 1 clearly states that the mitzvah applies to kosher animals such as cattle and sheep.
Question 2
Why did the Sages forbid tithing animals in the present era according to halacha 2?
The Sages were concerned about violating a severe prohibition by inadvertently eating an unblemished consecrated animal.
Question 3
What is the rule about selling a tithe animal that has become blemished, as per halacha 6?
Halacha 6 specifies that selling is primarily forbidden, but post-slaughter of a blemished tithe animal, exceptions exist.
Question 4
According to halacha 10, when are newly acquired lambs exempt from tithing?
If lambs are acquired, they are exempt from tithing because they did not originate in the buyer’s domain.
Question 5
What happens in cases of doubt regarding an animal's obligation to be tithed, as explained in halacha 16?
Halacha 16 clarifies that animals are exempt from tithing when there is doubt about their obligation.
