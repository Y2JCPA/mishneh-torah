הלכות בכורות פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
What happens to a blemished firstborn kosher animal?
H3 states that a blemished firstborn kosher animal is treated as ordinary property for the priest to eat or sell.
Question 2
What is required of a firstborn kosher animal in terms of sanctification?
H4 explains that a firstborn kosher animal becomes sanctified upon birth, though it's a mitzvah to declare it holy.
Question 3
Which offerings can be consumed outside Jerusalem?
According to H9 and H18, blemished firstlings can be eaten outside Jerusalem, while unblemished ones require Temple rituals.
Question 4
What must be done if a firstborn animal remains unblemished for a long period?
H11 allows for keeping the animal for two to three years, and a blemish must be verified by a sage.
Question 5
Are priests forced to eat firstborns within a certain timeframe?
H8 specifies that the firstborn should be eaten within the first year of life, whether blemished or not.
