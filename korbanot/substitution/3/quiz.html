הלכות תמורה פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
What happens if the holiness of a burnt-offering is transferred to a blemished animal?
Halacha 1 specifies that a blemished animal with transferred holiness must pasture until blemished again and then be sold.
Question 2
How is an animal with transferred holiness of a firstborn offering treated?
Halachot 1 and 2 explain that an animal with firstborn holiness is left to pasture, and then given to priests.
Question 3
What happens if a half consecrated animal's holiness is transferred?
According to Halacha 3, these animals must receive the same mixed status as the original consecrated animal.
Question 4
How are animals treated when holiness is transferred to a perpetually blemished animal?
Halacha 5 clarifies that although these animals are redeemed, they maintain a level of sanctity prohibiting shear and work.
Question 5
What is required when a burnt-offering and peace-offering become mixed?
Halacha 6 states that the mixture requires bringing new animals and making conditional offerings to clarify their status.
