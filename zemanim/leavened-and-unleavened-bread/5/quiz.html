הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which grains can become chametz?
Only five species can become chametz. Rice, corn, millet, and legumes cannot become chametz (though Ashkenazi custom avoids some of these as kitniyot).
Question 2
How quickly does dough become chametz?
18 minutes from when water contacts flour — if the dough sits undisturbed for this duration, it's chametz. This is why matzah bakeries work fast.
Question 3
Can rice matzah fulfill the mitzvah of eating matzah on Seder night?
The mitzvah requires matzah from grain that COULD become chametz but didn't. Rice can never become chametz, so rice matzah doesn't fulfill the obligation.
Question 4
A tiny crumb of chametz falls into a huge pot of Pesach soup. What happens?
Chametz on Pesach has no bitul — even the tiniest amount renders the entire mixture forbidden. This is uniquely strict; most forbidden foods DO have nullification in sufficient mixtures.
Question 5
Are legumes (beans, lentils) considered chametz?
Legumes are NOT chametz. Ashkenazi communities avoid kitniyot (legumes, rice, corn) on Pesach by custom — but this is a custom, not a Torah prohibition.
