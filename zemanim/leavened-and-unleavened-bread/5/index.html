The prohibition against chametz applies only to the five species of grain. They include two species of wheat: wheat and spelt; and three species of barley: barley, oats, and rye.However, kitniyot - e.g., rice, millet, beans, lentils and the like - do not become leavened. 🌾 The Five Grains & Leavening Agents
Chametz can only form from the five grain species: wheat, spelt, barley, oats, and rye. Kitniyot (rice, millet, legumes) cannot become chametz — they only decay. Fruit juice alone (wine, milk, honey, oil, fruit juices) never causes true leavening; only water does. Cooking flour directly in boiling water is technically permitted but forbidden by widespread custom. Roasted grain flour will not leaven, but lightly-roasted flour remains suspect.

Barley is soft and leavens rapidly — stirring it in water is forbidden; if kernels soften to the point of bursting under oven-heat, they become chametz. Dough must not exceed the challah-measure (~43⅕ eggs by volume). Only overnight-rested water may be used — not hot, sun-warmed, or freshly drawn. A woman must not knead in sun or under cloudy sky; she needs two water basins. Continuous kneading prevents leavening indefinitely; dough left to rest for the time to walk a mil (~18 min), or which produces a hollow clap, is chametz and burned. If two doughs rested simultaneously and one claps hollow, both are burned. Thick decorative loaves are forbidden for private persons. Spices and seeds may be added to dough; flour in charoset must be burned immediately. Seder matzah must use water only — no fruit juice — to recall 'poor man's bread.'

🫙 Purging Chametz from Vessels
Earthenware used for chametz while cold may only be used for cold matzah foods — except vessels for yeast, charoset, or leavened dough, which may never be used on Pesach. An earthenware roasting pan requires a thick clay lining and re-firing. Metal and stone vessels used as a kli rishon require hag'alah — submerging in boiling water — following 'as it absorbed, so it expels.' Vessels used only as a kli sheni need only be filled with boiling water. Earthenware used with hot chametz liquids cannot be kashered at all. A vessel too large to submerge is kashered by pouring boiling water over its rim.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 5
🌾
Only Five Grains Produce Chametz
Chametz comes exclusively from wheat, spelt, barley, oats, and rye. Rice, legumes, and other kitniyot cannot become chametz — they merely decay.
💧
Water Is the Critical Variable
Fruit juice alone never causes true leavening — only water does. The moment water is mixed with fruit juice in dough, leavening begins even faster than with water alone.
⏱️
Constant Motion Prevents Leavening
Dough under continuous agitation will not become chametz regardless of time. The danger begins only when dough rests: the benchmark is walking a mil (~18 minutes) or the appearance of a hollow-clap sound.
🔒
Shemirah — Watchfulness Over Matzah
The verse 'Keep watch over the matzot' obligates active supervision of grain intended for matzah, ideally from harvest. Grain wetted without supervision may render the matzah chametz.
