הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
When is bedikat chametz (the chametz search) conducted?
The night of the 14th — by candlelight. The search is done at night because candlelight reveals crevices better than daylight.
Question 2
What is bitul chametz?
Bitul: a verbal declaration nullifying ownership of all chametz you might have missed. "All chametz in my possession that I have not seen or removed — let it be null and void like dust."
Question 3
Are both bedikat chametz (search) and bitul (nullification) required?
The Rambam requires both: physical search and removal, plus verbal nullification. Belt and suspenders — ensuring no chametz remains, known or unknown.
Question 4
Do you need to check a bathroom for chametz?
Places where chametz is never brought don't need checking. The search targets places where chametz might actually be — not every square inch of the house.
Question 5
What do you do with the chametz found during the search?
Bi'ur chametz: burn the found chametz on the morning of the 14th, before the prohibition takes effect. Physical destruction ensures complete removal.
