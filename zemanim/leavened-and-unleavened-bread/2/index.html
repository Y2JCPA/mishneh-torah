📜 The Foundation
There is a positive Torah commandment to remove chametz before the time of its prohibition. Biblical removal means nullification in the heart — considering it as dust. The Rabbis additionally require searching and physically removing known chametz from one's domain, lest one come to eat it.

🕯️ Search by Candlelight
The search is performed only by candlelight — not by moonlight, sunlight, or torch. A hole in the middle of a wall is searched as far as the hand reaches; holes higher or lower than a handbreadth need not be searched. Concern that a mouse may have dragged chametz does not require re-searching unless there is clear evidence.

Indeed, other Rabbinic authorities (some of whom are quoted in the commentaries to the various halachot) devote much attention to this issue, clarifying at length whether the laws apply:a) only when a person had already nullified (or can still nullify) his chametz, or;b) even when the search for chametz is required by Torah law.Since the Rambam does not explicitly state his opinion on this matter, it is impossible to be sure which perspective he adopts. However, when the chapter is considered in its entirety, it appears that these halachot describe a situation where chametz has already been nullified. Unless specified to the contrary, this should be assumed to be the case. Nevertheless, certain of the leniencies are also applicable even when the search takes on the severity of a Torah obligation.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 2
📜
Nullification Is Biblical, Search Is Rabbinic
The Torah requires only nullification in the heart — considering chametz as dust. The Sages added physical search and removal to prevent accidental consumption of known chametz.
🕯️
Candlelight Is the Exclusive Search Tool
The search must be done specifically by candlelight, whose glow penetrates corners and crevices better than any large or diffuse light source.
⚖️
We Follow the Majority in Doubts
When nine piles are matzah and one is chametz, and a mouse took from the piles, we follow the majority — a core application of the principle of batar ruba.
🏠
Key Handover Determines Search Obligation
In rental situations, whoever holds the house at the start of the night of the 14th bears the obligation to search — whether landlord or tenant.
