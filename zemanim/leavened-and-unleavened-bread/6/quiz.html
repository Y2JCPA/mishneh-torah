הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
On which night(s) is eating matzah a Torah obligation?
The Torah obligation to eat matzah applies only on the first Seder night (15th of Nisan). After that, eating matzah is voluntary — you just can't eat chametz.
Question 2
What does "matzah shmurah" mean?
Shmurah = watched. The grain (or at minimum, the dough) is watched from the moment water contacts flour to ensure it doesn't sit long enough to become chametz.
Question 3
Can egg matzah (matzah ashirah) fulfill the Seder night obligation?
The Torah calls it "lechem oni" — bread of poverty/affliction. Enriched matzah made with eggs, juice, or oil is "rich bread" — it doesn't qualify for the mitzvah.
Question 4
What is the minimum amount of matzah required on Seder night?
A k'zayit (olive's bulk) is the minimum for fulfilling the mitzvah. Though custom is to eat more, the bare minimum obligation is one olive-sized piece.
Question 5
Must matzah be baked with specific intent for the mitzvah?
Lishmah is required: the matzah must be baked with conscious intent for the mitzvah of matzah. This is why the Rambam holds machine matzah may be problematic for the obligation.
