הלכה א׳
מִצְוַת עֲשֵׂה מִן הַתּוֹרָה לֶאֱכל מַצָּה בְּלֵיל חֲמִשָּׁה עָשָׂר שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר (שמות יב יח) "בָּעֶרֶב תֹּאכְלוּ מַצֹּת". בְּכָל מָקוֹם וּבְכָל זְמַן. וְלֹא תָּלָה אֲכִילָה זוֹ בְּקָרְבַּן הַפֶּסַח אֶלָּא זוֹ מִצְוָה בִּפְנֵי עַצְמָהּ וּמִצְוָתָהּ כָּל הַלַּיְלָה. אֲבָל בִּשְׁאָר הָרֶגֶל אֲכִילַת מַצָּה רְשׁוּת רָצָה אוֹכֵל מַצָּה רָצָה אוֹכֵל אֹרֶז אוֹ דֹּחַן אוֹ קְלָיוֹת אוֹ פֵּרוֹת. אֲבָל בְּלֵיל חֲמִשָּׁה עָשָׂר בִּלְבַד חוֹבָה. וּמִשֶּׁאָכַל כְּזַיִת יָצָא יְדֵי חוֹבָתוֹ:
It is a positive commandment of the Torah to eat matzah on the night of the fifteenth [of Nisan], as [Exodus 12:18] states: "In the evening, you shall eat matzot." This applies in every place and at every time. Eating [matzah] is not dependent on the Paschal sacrifice. Rather, it is a mitzvah in its own right. The mitzvah may be fulfilled throughout the entire night.Throughout the other days of the festival, eating matzah is left to one's choice: If one desires, one may eat matzah. If one desires, one may eat rice, millet, roasted seeds, or fruit. Nevertheless, on the night of the fifteenth alone, [eating matzah] is an obligation. Once one eats the size of an olive, he has fulfilled his obligation.

(It must be noted that the Pri Chadash and other commentaries do not accept this decision). 📜 A Positive Torah Commandment
Eating matzah on the night of the 15th of Nisan is a positive Torah commandment applicable everywhere and at all times — independent of the Pesach sacrifice. The obligation applies only on the first night; the remaining days of the festival are voluntary. One who swallows matzah without chewing — fulfills the obligation, though not ideally. One who eats matzah without intent to fulfill the mitzvah — e.g., if gentiles forced him to eat — fulfills the obligation after the fact.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 6
📜
An Independent Torah Obligation
Eating matzah on the night of the 15th is a Torah obligation in its own right — not dependent on the Pesach sacrifice — applicable everywhere and in every generation.
🌾
Only the Five Grain Species
Only matzah made from wheat, spelt, barley, oats, or rye fulfills the mitzvah; rice and other species are invalid.
🚫
Matzah Must Be Permissible to Eat
Matzah forbidden for any halachic reason — tevel, hekdesh, theft — is disqualified, derived from 'Guard the matzot' requiring matzah watched for the sake of the mitzvah.
⏰
Appetite and Aftertaste
No matzah on Erev Pesach ensures appetite at the Seder; nothing after the afikoman ensures the taste of matzah lingers in the mouth.
