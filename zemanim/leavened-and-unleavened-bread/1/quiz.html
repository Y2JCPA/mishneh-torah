הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the penalty for deliberately eating chametz on Pesach?
Karet: deliberately eating chametz on Pesach carries one of the Torah's most severe consequences.
Question 2
When does the Torah prohibition of chametz begin?
The Torah prohibits from midday on the 14th. The Sages extended the prohibition two hours earlier (from the 5th hour) as a protective buffer.
Question 3
What makes chametz unique among forbidden foods?
Most forbidden foods only prohibit eating. Chametz goes further: you can't eat it, own it, derive benefit from it, or even have it in your possession. Complete elimination.
Question 4
What is the minimum amount of chametz that triggers the prohibition?
A k'zayit (olive's bulk) triggers the full prohibition, including karet for deliberate consumption.
Question 5
Can you keep chametz in your house during Pesach if you don't plan to eat it?
The Torah prohibits owning chametz during Pesach — "no chametz shall be seen for you, no leaven shall be found." Ownership itself is forbidden, not just consumption.
