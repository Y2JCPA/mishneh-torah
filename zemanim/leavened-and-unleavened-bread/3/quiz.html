הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
You checked a room on the 12th of Nisan. Must you recheck on the night of the 14th?
A pre-checked room needs rechecking — between the original check and the 14th, chametz might have been introduced. The obligation is specifically for the night of the 14th.
Question 2
In a rented apartment, who is responsible for bedikat chametz?
Responsibility follows possession, not ownership. The tenant lives there and controls the space — the search obligation is theirs.
Question 3
You sell your chametz to a non-Jewish neighbor before Pesach. Is this effective?
Mechirat chametz works if it's a genuine sale — real transfer of ownership, accessible to the buyer, not a pretend legal fiction. The Rambam requires authenticity.
Question 4
A Jew rents their apartment to a non-Jew before Pesach. Does the space need bedikat chametz?
A non-Jew's domain doesn't require bedikat chametz. If genuine rental/possession transfers to the non-Jew, the Jewish owner has no obligation for that space.
Question 5
What principle determines who must search for chametz?
Possession determines obligation. The person who controls the space — has the keys, lives there, uses it — is responsible for the search, regardless of who owns the building.
