🕯️ Bedikat Chametz
On the night of the fourteenth, one must remove chametz from holes, hidden places, and corners. Chametz set aside to eat before morning must be kept securely so it does not scatter. If the fourteenth falls on Shabbat, the search is conducted on Thursday night (the thirteenth), and chametz is burned on Friday — but enough remains for two Shabbat meals and is nullified before the sixth hour on Friday.

הלכה ה׳

⚠️ Edge Cases
When one must burn pure and impure Terumah loaves on Erev Pesach that falls on Friday, they must be burned separately — pure loaves first — so that a kohen may still eat the pure ones if he arrives in time. One who forgot to search on the night of the fourteenth must search in the morning; if he also missed the morning, he searches during the holiday itself, leaving aside enough for that day's needs.

⏰ The Critical Deadline
A blessing is recited before searching even when fulfilling a Rabbinic commandment. After completing the search, one must nullify all unseen chametz in one's heart. This nullification must occur before the sixth hour on the fourteenth; one who fails to nullify by then has violated the positive commandment to remove chametz and must search and destroy any chametz found even during Pesach itself.

🔥 Destruction and Exemptions
One who leaves home to perform a mitzvah before the time to destroy chametz need not return — he may rely on nullification in his heart. If a rolled (unkneaded) dough was forgotten and he remembers while away, he should nullify it mentally; if he is before his teacher he should not interrupt — unless the dough will actually become chametz before he returns. Chametz must be destroyed by burning, crumbling and scattering to the wind, or throwing into the sea.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 3
🕯️
Search Everywhere
The bedikat chametz obligation extends to all holes, crevices, and hidden corners — a surface inspection is insufficient.
⏰
The Sixth Hour is the Hard Deadline
Nullification of chametz must be performed before the sixth hour on the fourteenth of Nisan. Failure to do so constitutes a biblical violation and requires search and destruction even during Pesach.
🤲
Blessings for Rabbinic Commands
A blessing is recited before searching for chametz even though bedikat chametz is a Rabbinic obligation — demonstrating that all Rabbinic commandments may carry a blessing.
🔥
Three Valid Methods of Destruction
Chametz may be destroyed by burning, crumbling and scattering to the wind, or casting into the sea — any method that renders it inaccessible fulfills the obligation.
