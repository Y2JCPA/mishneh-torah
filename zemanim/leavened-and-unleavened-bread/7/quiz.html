הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many cups of wine are required at the Seder?
Four cups corresponding to four expressions of redemption in Exodus. The Talmud insists: even a person living on charity must have four cups. Freedom has a non-negotiable minimum.
Question 2
What does charoset represent?
Charoset — a thick paste of fruits, nuts, and vinegar — looks like mortar. It's a sensory reminder of the bricks and mortar of Egyptian slavery.
Question 3
In which direction do you recline at the Seder?
Lean to the left — the direction that allows the right hand (dominant for most) to remain free for eating. Reclining embodies the freedom of the evening.
Question 4
Is the obligation to eat maror today Torah-level or rabbinic?
Today, without the Temple and the Pesach sacrifice, maror is a rabbinic obligation. When the Temple stood, it was Torah-level — paired with the korban Pesach.
Question 5
What is the central obligation of Seder night beyond the food?
Sippur yetziat Mitzrayim: telling the story. The food is the vehicle, but the story is the point. "Whoever expands on telling the Exodus story — this is praiseworthy."
