הלכות חמץ ומצה פרק ח · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the afikoman?
The afikoman is half of the middle matzah, hidden at the start of the Seder and eaten at the end. It must be the last food consumed — the taste of the Pesach sacrifice lingers.
Question 2
How many cups of wine are drunk during the Seder, and when?
Four cups at specific points: 1st at Kiddush, 2nd after Maggid, 3rd after Birkat Hamazon, 4th after Hallel. Each marks a transition in the Seder's narrative arc.
Question 3
What does "Seder" literally mean?
Seder means "order." The entire ceremony follows a precise sequence — each of the 15 steps is in a specific place for a reason.
Question 4
What is Korech (the Hillel sandwich)?
Korech: matzah + maror together in a sandwich. Hillel the Elder would combine matzah, maror, and the Pesach sacrifice in one bite. We follow his practice symbolically.
Question 5
Can you eat anything after the afikoman?
Nothing may be eaten after the afikoman — the taste of the Pesach sacrifice (represented by the afikoman matzah) must be the last taste. Only the final cups of wine follow.
