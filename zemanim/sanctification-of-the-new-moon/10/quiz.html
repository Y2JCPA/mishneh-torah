הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the interval between one season (tekufah) and the next according to Rav Ada?
Halacha 2 states the seasonal interval is 91 days, 7 hours, 519 parts, and 31 moments.
Question 2
When was Tekufat Nisan of the first year of creation relative to Molad Nisan?
Halacha 3: Tekufat Nisan of the first year of creation was 9 hours and 642 parts before Molad Nisan.
Question 3
What is the Rambam's personal opinion regarding which calculation the Sanhedrin used for intercalation?
In halacha 6, the Rambam writes 'nir'in li hadevarim' — the Sanhedrin relied on Rav Ada's calculation for intercalation because it is more astronomically accurate.
Question 4
What happens at the end of every 19-year cycle according to Rav Ada's calculation?
Halacha 1: at the end of every 19-year cycle there is no remainder at all — solar and lunar years align perfectly.
Question 5
By approximately how many days does the true Tekufat Nisan precede the calculated one (in both methods)?
Halacha 7: the sun's true position means Tekufat Nisan occurs about two days earlier than either Shmuel's or Rav Ada's calculation predicts.
