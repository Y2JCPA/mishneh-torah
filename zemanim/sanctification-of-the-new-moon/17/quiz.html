הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י״ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
When the moon is between Cancer and Sagittarius and the first longitude is 10° — what is the ruling?
Halacha 4: for Cancer through Sagittarius, first longitude of 10° or less means the moon certainly will not be seen anywhere in Eretz Yisrael.
Question 2
How many minutes are subtracted as the longitude parallax correction when the moon is in Taurus?
Halacha 5: when the moon is in Taurus, one full degree (60') is subtracted — the largest parallax correction, since Taurus is tilted significantly northward.
Question 3
In the 2 Iyar worked example, what was the arc of sighting?
Halachot 13-14: after all calculation stages — fourth longitude 13°46' minus geographic latitude correction 2°35' — the arc of sighting came to 11°11'.
Question 4
When the arc of sighting is 12°10', what is the minimum first longitude required for visibility?
Halacha 19: when the arc is between 12° and 13°, the first longitude must be 10° or more for the moon to be visible.
Question 5
What does the Rambam teach at the chapter's conclusion about the source of astronomical wisdom?
Halacha 24: the Rambam rules that anything demonstrated with clear evidence is accepted — whether stated by a prophet or a gentile. We rely on proofs and reasoning, not the author's identity.
