הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י״ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
If the solar course is 250°, how do you find its correction?
For courses above 180°, subtract from 360°: 360-250=110°. The correction for 110° is 1°53'. This is ruled in halacha 5.
Question 2
What happens when the solar course is exactly 180°?
The Rambam rules explicitly in halacha 3: when the course is exactly 180°, there is no correction, and the mean position equals the true position.
Question 3
In the example of 14 Tammuz, the course was 18°52'2''. What course value did the Rambam use?
Since 52' is greater than 30', it counts as an additional full degree — course = 19°, with a correction of 38'. This is explicit in halacha 9.
Question 4
What is the maximum correction value in the table, and at what course does it occur?
According to the Rambam's table in halacha 4: course 90° has a correction of 1°59', which is the maximum value.
Question 5
How are seconds handled in calculating the sun's true position?
The Rambam rules in halacha 10: do not consider seconds at all — not for the sun, moon, or any visibility calculations. Only if close to 30, round to one additional part.
