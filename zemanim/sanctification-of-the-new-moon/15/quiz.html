הלכות קידוש החודש פרק ט״ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the range of double elongation on the night of potential moon sighting?
The Rambam rules in halacha 2 that the double elongation on the sighting night is never less than 5° and never more than 62°.
Question 2
If the double elongation is 35°, how many degrees are added to the mean anomaly?
35° falls in the range 32°-38°, so 5° is added to the mean anomaly (halacha 3).
Question 3
What happens when the corrected anomaly is exactly 180°?
The Rambam rules in halacha 5 that a corrected anomaly of exactly 180° or 360° has no correction at all — the mean lunar position is itself the true position.
Question 4
What is the correction value when the corrected anomaly is 90°?
According to the table in halacha 6, a corrected anomaly of 90° yields a correction of 5°5'. The maximum (5°8') occurs at 100°.
Question 5
In the 2 Iyar example, what is the moon's true position at sighting time?
After all computation steps (halachot 8-9): double elongation 35°56', corrected anomaly 108°, correction 5°1' — subtracted from mean moon yields 18°36' in Taurus.
