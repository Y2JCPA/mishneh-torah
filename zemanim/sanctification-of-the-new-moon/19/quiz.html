הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י״ט · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many degrees from the equator is the start of Cancer (maximum northern declination)?
The Rambam states that the start of Cancer is approximately 23.5° north of the equator — the maximum declination, mirrored by Capricorn at 23.5° south.
Question 2
Where does the moon appear when it is 14° north of the equator?
Halacha 13: when the moon is north of the equator, it appears between west and north, with its concavity tilting toward the southeast.
Question 3
What do you do when the moon's zodiac declination and its latitude are in opposite directions?
Halacha 10: when declination and latitude are in different directions (one north, one south), subtract the smaller from the larger. The result is in the direction of the larger value.
Question 4
How do you find the declination for a position between 180° and 270°?
Halacha 9: for positions above 180° up to 270°, subtract 180° from the value, and the remainder gives the equivalent declination.
Question 5
What determines how high the moon appears to witnesses?
Halacha 15: a short sighting arc means the moon appears low near the horizon; a long arc means it appears high — this is what witnesses report about the moon's height.
