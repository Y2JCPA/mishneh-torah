הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the sun's mean daily motion according to the Rambam?
The Rambam states explicitly that the sun's mean daily motion is 59 parts and 8 seconds of arc.
Question 2
How many days are there from one new moon sighting night to the next?
The Rambam emphasizes: from one sighting night to the next is always exactly 29 complete days — this is why 29-day lookup tables are essential.
Question 3
What was the sun's apogee position at the epoch (3 Nisan 4938)?
The epoch records the apogee at 26° 45' 8'' in Gemini, while the mean solar position was 7° 3' 32'' in Aries.
Question 4
How fast does the solar apogee move?
The Rambam writes that the apogee moves one degree approximately every seventy years, which works out to 53 seconds per regular year.
Question 5
A leap year with full months exceeds a regular year by how many days?
The Rambam specifies: a leap year with regular months exceeds by 30 days; with full months by 31 days; with deficient months by 29 days.
