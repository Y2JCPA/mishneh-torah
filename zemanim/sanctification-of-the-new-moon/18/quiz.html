הלכות קידוש החודש פרק י״ח · 5 Questions
Question 1
During the Sanhedrin era, what is the minimum number of full (30-day) months in a year?
The Rambam rules in halacha 9 that there are never fewer than 4 full months per year and never more than 8 — when sanctifying by witnesses.
Question 2
When the sighting arc is 9°5' and the first longitude is 13°, and witnesses come in summer from low ground — what does the court do?
Halacha 4: when the arc is short and calculation gives marginal visibility — in summer or from low ground, the court suspects the testimony and investigates thoroughly.
Question 3
What did R' Yehuda HaNasi do based on the Sinai tradition about calculated months?
Halacha 11: R' Yehuda HaNasi made 9 months deficient (29-day) in a single year, based on the oral tradition from Moses about calculated alternation of full and deficient months.
Question 4
If the moon is seen in a land east of Eretz Yisrael, what can be concluded about EY?
Halacha 15: if the moon was seen in an eastern land, it was certainly seen in Eretz Yisrael — since the moon sets later in the west, EY had even more opportunity to see it.
Question 5
What is the Rambam's bottom line about the geographic sighting rules for east and west of EY?
Halacha 16: all these geographic rules are stated 'lehagdil Torah ulha'adirah' — purely to magnify Torah. In practice, no community east or west ever relied on their own sighting; all relied solely on the court in Eretz Yisrael.
