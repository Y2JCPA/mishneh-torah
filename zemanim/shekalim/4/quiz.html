הלכות שקלים פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
What funds the Sanctuary curtains (between the Holy and Holy of Holies)?
The Sanctuary curtains are structural (in the First Temple there was a wall), so they are funded from Temple maintenance, not from the Terumah.
Question 2
How much did judges adjudicating robbery cases in Jerusalem receive per year?
The Rambam rules ninety maneh per year, and if insufficient, more was added for their needs, wives, and children.
Question 3
Why was a volunteer offering to guard Shemittah aftergrowth for free refused?
The Sages feared that powerful men would come and seize the produce. A paid guard from the Treasury makes it publicly known that the field is officially guarded, deterring abuse.
Question 4
What is done with surplus incense at the end of the fiscal year?
The incense's sanctity is transferred onto artisans' wages (the money goes to summer of the altar), the artisans take the incense as wages, then the court buys it back from them with new Terumah funds.
Question 5
If shekels were insufficient for communal offerings, where are supplementary funds drawn from?
The Rambam rules that shortfalls are covered from Temple maintenance funds. However, the reverse is never permitted — altar funds may not be used for Temple maintenance.
