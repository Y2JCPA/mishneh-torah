הלכות שקלים פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
Two people who sent one shekel for both of them — what is their kalbon obligation?
Two who gave one shekel for both owe one kalbon, since each owes a half-shekel and their combination into one shekel requires the exchange surcharge (Halacha 1).
Question 2
What is the amount of the kalbon when the half-shekel equals two dinars?
The kalbon is half a ma'ah — one-twelfth of a dinar. This is the minimum established amount (Halacha 7).
Question 3
A city sent shekels with a paid guardian and bandits seized them by force — who is liable?
A paid guardian is exempt from force majeure. Therefore the city must pay their shekels again, since responsibility remains on them until the funds reach the treasurer (Halacha 9).
Question 4
One designated a shekel for his obligation, then discovered he was not obligated — what is the status?
Designation in error does not sanctify. Since he thought he was obligated but was not, the sanctity does not take hold and the shekel remains ordinary money (Halacha 12).
Question 5
Coins found exactly halfway between the wood box and the frankincense box — where do they go?
When found exactly halfway between two boxes, the coins go to the more stringent category. Frankincense is more stringent than wood, so they go to frankincense (Halacha 15).
