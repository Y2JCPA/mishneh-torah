הלכות שקלים פרק ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many boxes were placed in each city for shekel collection, and why?
Each city had two boxes: one for the current year's shekels and one for the previous year, to collect from those who hadn't given last year.
Question 2
How many times per year was the Terumah drawn from the chamber?
The Terumah was drawn three times: Rosh Chodesh Nisan, around Rosh HaShanah, and fifteen days before Shavuot.
Question 3
Why was the last large container drawn from left uncovered?
The last container was left uncovered so they would know to begin drawing from it the next time they entered to take the Terumah.
Question 4
The Terumah's intent encompasses which funds?
The intent covers all three: what is already in the chamber, what has been collected but not yet arrived, and what will be collected in the future — so all Israel shares in the atonement.
Question 5
Who is forbidden from drawing the Terumah even with all precautions in place?
Even with all precautions, a poor person or one greedy for wealth may not draw the Terumah — to prevent suspicion, as it says: 'You shall be clean before God and before Israel.'
