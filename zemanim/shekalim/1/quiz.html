הלכות שקלים פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
A poor person supported by charity — what is his half-shekel obligation?
The Rambam rules explicitly that even a poor person on charity is obligated — he borrows from others or sells his garment.
Question 2
From what date in Adar does coercion and pledging begin for non-payers?
On the 25th of Adar, tables were set up in the Temple, and from then on collectors would coerce and even take a person's garment by force.
Question 3
A Cuthean (Kuti) gives a half-shekel — what is done?
Unlike women, minors, and slaves whose voluntary contributions are accepted — from a Cuthean it is never accepted at all.
Question 4
A father who began giving the half-shekel on behalf of his minor son — what is the ruling?
Once a father begins giving for his son, he may not stop — he continues each year until the son comes of age and gives on his own.
Question 5
When the current coin was a darkon (= 2 selaim), how much was given as the half-shekel?
Since the darkon was the current coin, one gave its half — one sela — as the half-shekel.
