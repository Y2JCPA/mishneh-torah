הלכות שופר וסוכה ולולב פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the halachic status of a stolen shofar that was sounded on Rosh HaShanah?
A stolen shofar is valid because the mitzvah consists solely of hearing the sound, not in ownership of the shofar (Halacha 3).
Question 2
If a shofar is coated with gold on the outside, when is it still valid?
External coating invalidates the shofar only if it changes the sound. If the sound is unchanged, it remains kosher (Halacha 6).
Question 3
What is the minimum size requirement for a kosher shofar?
The shofar must be large enough that when held, both ends protrude visibly on either side — approximately one expanded handbreadth (Halacha 5).
Question 4
In the Temple on Rosh HaShanah, what was the relationship between the shofar and the trumpets?
The shofar sounded long while the two flanking trumpets sounded short, because the primary mitzvah of the day belongs to the shofar (Halacha 2).
Question 5
A person outside a pit hears only an echo of the shofar blast — has he fulfilled his obligation?
One who hears only an echo does not fulfill the obligation. The mitzvah requires hearing the genuine shofar blast, not its reflection (Halacha 8).
