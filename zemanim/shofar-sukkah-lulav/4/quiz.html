הלכות שופר וסוכה ולולב פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the minimum required height of a sukkah's interior space?
Halacha 1: the interior height must be no less than ten handbreadths — anything less is not considered a dwelling fit for habitation.
Question 2
A hanging wall is valid only if it is within how many handbreadths of the ground?
Halacha 4: a hanging wall more than three handbreadths above the earth is invalid; within three handbreadths it is acceptable via lavud.
Question 3
When two full walls run parallel with an open gap, what is the minimum width of the third stub wall?
Halacha 3: the third wall must be slightly more than four handbreadths — just enough to be halachically recognized as a wall.
Question 4
How can a sukkah taller than twenty cubits be made valid?
Halacha 14: a bench at least seven handbreadths wide, extending the full span of the middle wall, lowers the effective interior height and renders the sukkah valid.
Question 5
Is a sukkah built on the deck of a ship valid?
Halacha 6: Rabbi Akiva's view prevailed after observing Rabban Gamliel using such a sukkah on a voyage — a shipboard sukkah is kosher.
