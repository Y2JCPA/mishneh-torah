הלכות שופר וסוכה ולולב פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the maximum width of boards that may be used as valid s'chach?
Halacha 6: boards less than four handbreadths wide may be used as s'chach. Four handbreadths or more is considered a significant structure and is invalid.
Question 2
When is a sukkah under a tree still valid?
Halacha 11: a sukkah under a tree is like one built indoors, but if the open (unshaded) area exceeds the shade, it may still be kosher.
Question 3
A cloth is spread below the s'chach to catch falling leaves — what is the ruling?
Halacha 16: spreading a cloth either above or below the s'chach invalidates the sukkah, as it interposes between the person and the kosher s'chach.
Question 4
A bed canopy inside a sukkah is exactly ten handbreadths high — what is the ruling?
Halacha 22: a canopy of ten handbreadths or more constitutes an independent structure, rendering the space below it invalid as a sukkah.
Question 5
May one fulfill the sukkah mitzvah in a borrowed sukkah?
Halacha 24: a borrowed sukkah is entirely valid. Unlike a lulav on the first day, a sukkah is acquired through residence — no ownership transfer needed.
