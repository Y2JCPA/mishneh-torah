הלכות שופר וסוכה ולולב פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many shofar blasts is one obligated to hear on Rosh HaShanah by Torah law?
Nine blasts are required — three teru'ot, each flanked by a teki'ah before and after (3×3=9), corresponding to the three mentions of 'teru'ah' in the Torah.
Question 2
One heard nine blasts from nine people blowing simultaneously — what is the ruling?
He does not fulfill even one blast, because the sounds blend together and one cannot distinguish between teki'ah and teru'ah.
Question 3
The three middle blessings — Malchuyot, Zichronot, and Shofarot — are they interdependent?
These three blessings are interdependent — if one is omitted, the obligation for the others is not fulfilled either.
Question 4
How many verses must be included in each of the blessings of Malchuyot, Zichronot, and Shofarot?
Each blessing requires at least ten verses — three from the Torah, three from Writings, three from Prophets, and a concluding one from the Torah.
Question 5
The person who blew the seated blasts spoke between the seated and standing sets — what is the ruling?
Although one should not speak between the two sets, after the fact the obligation is fulfilled and no new blessing is needed, since both sets are part of one mitzvah.
