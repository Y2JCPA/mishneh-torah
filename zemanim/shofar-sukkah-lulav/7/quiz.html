הלכות שופר וסוכה ולולב פרק ז · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many myrtle branches are required for the lulav mitzvah?
Halacha 7: one lulav, one etrog, two willow branches, and three myrtle branches. Additional myrtle may be added for beauty, but the minimum is three.
Question 2
What is the minimum length of a lulav, and from where is it measured?
Halacha 8: the lulav must be at least four handbreadths, measured from its spine only, not from the leaf tips. Myrtle and willow — three handbreadths.
Question 3
Who enacted that the lulav be taken all seven days everywhere after the Temple's destruction, and why?
Halacha 15: after the destruction, Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai enacted that the lulav be taken everywhere all seven days as a memorial to the Temple.
Question 4
Is it permitted to smell the myrtle in the lulav?
Halacha 26: the myrtle in the lulav is forbidden to smell because it is suited only for fragrance, and since it is designated for the mitzvah, it is forbidden. The etrog, by contrast, may be smelled since it is suited for eating.
Question 5
When the first day of Sukkot falls on Shabbat in our time, what is the ruling regarding the lulav?
Halacha 18: in our time, with the fixed calendar, the lulav is not taken on Shabbat at all — not in the diaspora, not in Eretz Yisrael, even on the first day.
