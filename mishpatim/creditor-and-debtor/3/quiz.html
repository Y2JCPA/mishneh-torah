🎓 Quiz

הלכות מלוה ולווה פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
Why is a widow's garment never taken as collateral?
Deuteronomy 24:17 states: 'Do not take the garment of a widow as collateral.' This applies whether she is rich or poor.
Question 2
How many separate Torah violations occur when one takes food-producing utensils as collateral?
The Rambam rules that each food utensil taken is a separate violation — the verse mentions upper and lower millstones to establish they are counted independently.
Question 3
What is the difference between a creditor and a court agent when collecting collateral?
Both must stand outside, but the court agent may then forcibly take what the debtor brings. The creditor must wait for voluntary delivery.
Question 4
Of what benefit is collateral for a poor debtor's item that must be returned whenever he needs it?
The collateral keeps the debt alive past the Shemitah year and allows collection from the estate after the debtor dies, even though it must be returned during his lifetime.
Question 5
Who is permitted to forcibly enter a borrower's home and take collateral?
The guarantor may enter and seize collateral because Proverbs 20:16 states 'Take his garment because he guaranteed a stranger.'
