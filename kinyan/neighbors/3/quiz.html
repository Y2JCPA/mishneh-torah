הלכות שכנים פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
When a shared dividing wall falls, who is responsible for rebuilding it?
When a shared wall falls, both partners may compel each other to share in rebuilding it — the obligation is mutual.
Question 2
If a person builds a wall on his own property (not the shared boundary), what does the neighbor owe?
When a wall is built on the builder's own land, the Rambam rules the neighbor pays only a modest sum for the benefit received — not the full half.
Question 3
When five gardens all draw water from one spring and the spring becomes impaired, how do the owners draw water?
Water rights are allocated by proximity — the field closest to the spring draws first, then the next, in order. This applies even when the spring is damaged.
Question 4
What height must a roof guard rail be built to for a roof near a neighbor's courtyard?
A roof guard rail near a neighbor's courtyard must be four cubits high — the same standard as a courtyard dividing wall.
Question 5
When a river flows next to several fields and owners irrigate from it, how is the order determined?
Owners irrigate in sequence — each in his proper turn as the river flows past their fields, from the first to the last.
