הלכות שכנים פרק ט · 5 Questions
Question 1
How far must a cistern or pit be from a neighbor's wall?
A cistern or pit must be distanced at least 3 handbreadths from a neighbor's wall — and the surrounding soil must be plastered with lime to prevent seepage.
Question 2
Why must a ladder be kept 4 cubits away from a neighbor's dovecote?
The 4-cubit distance prevents a marten (a weasel-like animal) from using the ladder as a bridge to reach and kill the neighbor's doves in the dovecote.
Question 3
How far must a laundry beating stone be from a neighbor's wall?
A laundry stone used for beating clothes must be separated by 4 cubits from a neighbor's wall — the impact vibrations can damage the wall.
Question 4
What must an oven owner do to comply with distance requirements from a neighbor's wall?
An oven must be distanced 3 handbreadths from the wall and sufficiently insulated — both distance and insulation are required to prevent heat damage.
Question 5
What happens if the owner of a second floor makes many windows in the floor, causing the first-floor ceiling to weaken?
The second-floor owner may not make so many windows that the floor/ceiling weakens — he has an obligation not to structurally compromise the property of the first-floor owner below him.
