הלכות ביכורים פרק ה · 5 Questions
Question 1
According to Scriptural law, what is the minimum amount of dough that satisfies the challah obligation?
Halachah 1 states that according to Scriptural Law there is no minimum measure — even a barley-corn's worth absolves the entire dough. The 1/24 and 1/48 measures are Rabbinic standards instituted to ensure a meaningful gift to the priest.
Question 2
Why does a commercial baker separate only 1/48 of the dough rather than the standard 1/24?
Halachah 2 explains that since a baker's dough is larger, even 1/48 of it amounts to a meaningful present for the priest, satisfying the requirement to 'give him' something worthy of being called a gift.
Question 3
In the territory from Kziv to Amanah, why are two challot separated rather than one?
Halachah 8 explains that this land lost its sanctification after the exile, making the first challah impure and requiring it to be burnt. A second challah is added for the priest to eat so people will not mistakenly conclude that pure terumah may be burnt.
Question 4
Which type of ritual impurity bars a priest from eating Diaspora challah, but does NOT bar him from eating challah in the present era in Eretz Yisrael?
Halachah 10 states that Diaspora challah (purely Rabbinic) is forbidden only to priests impure due to bodily conditions (zav, zavah, niddah, etc.). Corpse-impurity alone does not bar them. Halachah 8 indicates that in Eretz Yisrael even those impure from bodily conditions may partake of the challah given to the priest.
Question 5
Why may a blind person separate challah as an initial preference, even though a blind person may not initially separate terumah?
Halachah 15 explains that terumah must be separated from the superior produce, so a blind person — unable to identify it — should not separate terumah initially. But challah involves no such superior/inferior distinction among the dough, so a blind person may separate it even at the outset.
