הלכות ביכורים פרק י · 5 Questions
Question 1
Where does the mitzvah of first shearings apply?
Rambam rules that the first shearings apply only in Eretz Yisrael, and unlike some Temple-dependent mitzvot, this one applies even today — similar to terumah.
Question 2
An animal was permanently blemished BEFORE being consecrated to the Temple treasury, then redeemed. What is the law regarding its first shearings?
Since a permanently blemished animal was never fit for the altar at the time of consecration, the consecration did not affect its essential substance. Once redeemed, it returns fully to ordinary status and is obligated in first shearings.
Question 3
A Jew purchases only the shearing rights to a gentile's flock and shears the sheep himself. Is he obligated in the first shearings?
The obligation attaches at the moment of shearing. Since the shearer is Jewish and the shearings belong to him, he is obligated — even though the sheep revert to the gentile after shearing.
Question 4
What is the minimum requirement for a flock to be obligated in first shearings?
Both conditions must be met: at least five sheep in the flock, each contributing a minimum of 12 selaim, and the combined total must reach 60 selaim — only then is the gift significant enough to make a small garment.
Question 5
A person declares: 'Let all of my shearings be considered first shearings.' What is the legal effect?
Unlike terumah (which implies separating a portion from a larger whole), the term 'first shearings' carries no such connotation. Therefore, one may designate his entire shearing as first shearings and his declaration is effective.
