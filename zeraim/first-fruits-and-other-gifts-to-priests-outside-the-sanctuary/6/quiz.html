לרפואת פייגא בת יטא רבקה

🎓 Quiz

הלכות ביכורים פרק ו · 5 Questions
Question 1
From which of the following is challah NOT required to be separated?
Halachah 12 explicitly exempts dough cooked in a pot from challah, because it is considered cooked food rather than bread. Dough baked in any vessel — oven, pit, or pan — is obligated.
Question 2
What is the minimum measure of dough from which challah must be separated?
Halachah 15 establishes the minimum measure as one omer, derived from the omer of manna allotted per person in the desert (Exodus 16:16), equivalent in volume to 43⅕ eggs.
Question 3
A baker bakes many small loaves, each below the minimum measure, and places them in a basket. What is the law?
Halachah 16 rules that a basket joins the loaves together to create a challah obligation, derived from the verse 'the bread of the land.' However, an oven does NOT join loaves — only a containing vessel does.
Question 4
When a gentile separates challah from dough in Eretz Yisrael, what is the ruling?
Halachah 10 rules that when a gentile separates challah, it has no sanctity whatsoever and may be eaten by a non-priest, because challah is a Jewish obligation that does not apply to gentiles.
Question 5
If a baker makes a dough with the explicit intent to divide it while still raw dough (not to bake it), what is the law?
Halachah 19 rules that a dough made to be divided while still raw dough is exempt from challah, because challah applies only when the dough may ultimately be baked as bread. Since he intends to distribute it as raw dough, there is no obligation.
