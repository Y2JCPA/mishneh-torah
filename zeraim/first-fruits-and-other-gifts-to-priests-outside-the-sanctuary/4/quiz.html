לרפואת פייגא בת יטא רבקה

🎓 Quiz

הלכות ביכורים פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which of the following individuals must bring first fruits but may NOT recite the declaration?
A guardian cannot say 'the land You gave me' because the land belongs to his ward, not to him. Converts, priests, and Levites all bring AND declare, for each has a legitimate claim tied to the covenant or to God's gift of dwelling cities.
Question 2
A person buys two trees (but not the land) in a neighbor's field and brings their fruit as first fruits. What does he do?
Because there is doubt whether he owns the land under the trees, the produce must first be consecrated, then sent via an agent (so no declaration is expected), and tithes given specifically to priests — in case the fruits are actually first fruits forbidden to non-priests.
Question 3
A farmer sets aside first fruits, then sells his field. He later brings the separated fruits to Jerusalem. What is the law?
He brings them — his separation was valid — but he cannot say 'the land You gave me' because it has since been sold. The purchaser need not separate again from the same species, since the seller already separated for it.
Question 4
Someone brings first fruits between Sukkot and Chanukah (after the holiday of Sukkot has ended). What is the correct procedure?
Deuteronomy 26:11 ties the declaration to a time of 'all the good' — the season of joy from Shavuot through Sukkot. After Sukkot ends, one may still bring until Chanukah, but without the declaration. After Chanukah, they may not be brought at all.
Question 5
What leads the procession of first-fruit bringers as they ascend to Jerusalem?
An ox with its horns glazed with gold leads the procession, with a crown of olive branches on its head to publicize that the first fruits come from the seven species for which Eretz Yisrael is praised. A flute plays for the whole procession, but it is the adorned ox that leads.
