הלכות ביכורים פרק י״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
When beheading a firstling donkey that will not be redeemed, which instrument does the Rambam specify as required?
Halachah 7 specifies a butcher's hatchet (striking from behind the neck). Killing the animal with a staff, reed, axe, or saw is invalid — and one may not simply lock it in a room to die.
Question 2
From when does the obligation to redeem a firstling donkey begin, and when does delay in the mitzvah begin?
Halachah 6 states the obligation begins from birth. After 30 days, one may still redeem or behead, but there has been a delay in fulfilling the mitzvah — unlike a firstborn son who may not be redeemed until after 30 days.
Question 3
Which of the following is invalid for redeeming a firstling donkey?
Halachah 8 explicitly excludes a calf, wild beast, slaughtered animal, deathly ill animal, hybrid, and ko'i. Only a living sheep or goat qualifies. A blemished sheep (H10), small lamb (H10), and a seh resembling another species (H9, after the fact) are all valid.
Question 4
A gentile owns one-thousandth of the mother of a firstling donkey. What is the halachic status of the firstling?
Halachah 15 rules that if a gentile owns even one-thousandth of the mother or the offspring, the animal is entirely exempt from the firstling obligations — the Torah requires it to 'entirely belong to an Israelite.'
Question 5
Two donkeys, neither of which had given birth before, both give birth to males. How many lambs must the owner give to a priest?
Halachah 20 rules that when two first-time donkeys both give birth to males, both males are certainly firstborns. The owner must give two lambs to the priest — there is no doubt involved.
