🤝 Covenant of Salt
The 24 priestly gifts were established through a covenant of salt with Aaron — as enduring as salt itself, never spoiling. Any priest who denies them forfeits his share in the priesthood entirely. When eating a sanctified gift, a priest recites a special blessing invoking the sanctity of Aaron. The Rambam then maps five distinct categories that will organize the entire chapter.

🗺️ Where You Eat = How Holy
Location of consumption mirrors sanctity. Eight gifts (sin-offerings, guilt-offerings, communal peace-offerings, omer remnants, meal-offering remnants, two Shavuot loaves, show bread, metzora's oil log) must be eaten inside the Temple Courtyard. Five gifts (breast/leg of peace-offering, thanksgiving and nazirite portions, firstborn animal, first fruits) are eaten anywhere in Jerusalem. Five gifts (terumah, terumat ma'aser, challah, first shearings, ancestral field) apply only in Eretz Yisrael. Five gifts (slaughter presents, firstborn-son redemption, firstborn-donkey redemption, convert's stolen property, dedication offerings) apply everywhere. One unique gift — the hides of burnt-offerings — comes from Temple property itself.

הלכה י׳

הִנֵּה לָמַדְתָּ שֶׁהַמַּתָּנוֹת שֶׁנִּתָּנִין לִנְקֵבוֹת כִּזְכָרִים חָמֵשׁ מַתָּנוֹת. תְּרוּמָה. וּתְרוּמַת מַעֲשֵׂר. וְחַלָּה. וּמַתְּנוֹת בְּהֵמָה. וְרֵאשִׁית הַגֵּז. וּמִנַּיִן אַתָּה אוֹמֵר שֶׁרֵאשִׁית הַגֵּז נִתֶּנֶת לַכֹּהֲנוֹת שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר (דברים יח ד) "רֵאשִׁית דְּגָנְךָ תִּירשְׁךָ וְיִצְהָרֶךָ וְרֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ". מָה רֵאשִׁית דָּגָן נִתֶּנֶת לִנְקֵבוֹת כִּזְכָרִים אַף רֵאשִׁית הַגֵּז:
Thus you have learned that the presents given [directly] to females like males are five: terumah,42See HilchotTerumah 12:22. terumat ma'aser, challah,43For the laws applying to terumah apply to both of these. the presents given when slaughtering an animal,44The foreleg, the jaw, and the maw; see Chapter 9, Halachah 1. and the first shearings.What is the source that teaches that the first shearings may be given to a woman of the priestly family? 'The first of your grain, your wine, and your oil, and the first shearings of your flock shall you give to him' [Deuteronomy 18:4]. [The verse establishes an equivalence:] Just as [terumah,] the first of the grain, may be given to females as well as males, so too, the first shearings.
👨‍👩‍👧 Males, Females, Watches
The eight Temple gifts (most-holy order) go exclusively to male priests, as the verse states: 'All males among the priesthood shall partake of it.' The five Jerusalem gifts (lesser sanctity) go to both males and females of the priestly family, but are distributed through the priestly watch on duty that week. Five gifts — terumah, terumat ma'aser, challah, the slaughtering presents, and first shearings — are given equally to female priests, derived from a scriptural parallel between 'first of the grain' and 'first shearings.'

The Rabbinic Reckoning: An Alternative Count of 24

הלכות י״ב–ט״ו
הלכה י״ב׳
מָנוּ חֲכָמִים מַתָּנוֹת אֵלּוּ עַל דֶּרֶךְ אַחֶרֶת וְאָמְרוּ עֶשְׂרִים וְאַרְבַּע מַתְּנוֹת כְּהֻנָּה נִתְּנוּ לְאַהֲרֹן וְאֵלּוּ הֵן. עֶשֶׂר בַּמִּקְדָּשׁ. אַרְבַּע בִּירוּשָׁלַיִם. וְעֶשֶׂר בַּגְּבוּלִין:
Our Sages45Tosefta, Challah 2:7. reckoned these presents in another manner, stating: 24 presents for the priests were given to Aaron. They are 10 in the Temple, 4 in Jerusalem, and 10 in the outlying areas.
הלכה י״ג׳
עֶשֶׂר בַּמִּקְדָּשׁ. חַטַּאת בְּהֵמָה. וְחַטַּאת הָעוֹף. אָשָׁם. וְאָשָׁם תָּלוּי. זִבְחֵי שַׁלְמֵי צִבּוּר. וְלֹג שֶׁמֶן שֶׁל מְצֹרָע. שְׁתֵּי הַלֶּחֶם. וְלֶחֶם הַפָּנִים. שְׁיָרֵי מְנָחוֹת. וּמוֹתַר הָעֹמֶר:
The ten in the Temple are: a) an animal brought as a sin-offering, b) a fowl brought as a sin-offering, c) a guilt-offering, d) a guilt-offering brought because of doubt, e) the communal peace-offerings, f) the log of oil brought by a nazirite, g) the two breads, h) the show bread, i) the remnants of the meal offering, and j) the remnants of the omer offering.
הלכה י״ד׳
אַרְבַּע בִּירוּשָׁלַיִם. הַבְּכוֹר. וְהַבִּכּוּרִים. וּמוּרָם מִתּוֹדָה וְאֵיל נָזִיר. וְעוֹרוֹת קָדָשִׁים:
The four in Jerusalem are: a) the firstborn [kosher animal], b) the first fruits, c) the portion granted [the priests] from the thanksgiving offering and the portion granted [the priests] from the ram brought by a nazirite, and d) the hides of the sacrifices.46Although these hides are not eaten in Jerusalem, but instead, can be sold and the proceeds used for any purpose, the Rambam places them in this category, because the priests would not take them out of the holy city. Instead, as a token of respect for their holiness, they would sell them there and use the proceeds to partake of food that was eaten in that holy place (Radbaz, gloss to Halachah 5).
הלכה ט״ו׳
עֶשֶׂר בַּגְּבוּלִין. תְּרוּמָה. וּתְרוּמַת מַעֲשֵׂר. וְחַלָּה. וְרֵאשִׁית הַגֵּז. וְהַמַּתָּנוֹת. וּפִדְיוֹן הַבֵּן. וּפִדְיוֹן פֶּטֶר חֲמוֹר. שְׂדֵה חֲרָמִים. וּשְׂדֵה אֲחֻזָּה. וְגֵזֶל הַגֵּר. וּלְפִי חֶשְׁבּוֹן זֶה חַשֵּׁב כָּל הַמּוּרָם מִקָּדָשִׁים קַלִּים מַתָּנָה אַחַת. וְהֵן חָזֶה וְשׁוֹק הַמּוּרָם מִכָּל זֶבַח שְׁלָמִים עִם הַלֶּחֶם הַמּוּרָם עִמָּהֶם [אִם הָיָה תּוֹדָה. עִם הַזְּרוֹעַ הַמּוּרֶמֶת מֵאֵיל נָזִיר עִם הַלֶּחֶם הַמּוּרָם עִמָּהּ] עִם חָזֶה וְשׁוֹק. הוֹאִיל וּשְׁלָשְׁתָּן שְׁלָמִים הֵן חֲשֹׁב הַמּוּרָם מֵהֶם מַתָּנָה אַחַת:
The ten in the outlying areas are: a) terumah, b) terumat ma'aser, c) challah, d) the first shearings, e) the presents given when slaughtering an animal, f) the redemption of a firstborn [son], g) the redemption of a firstborn donkey, h) a field that was designated as a dedication offering, i) an ancestral field, and j) the stolen property of a convert.According to this reckoning, all of the portions given the priests from sacrifices of lesser sanctity are considered as one present. This includes: the breast and the leg that is given [the priest] from every peace offering, together with the bread that is given with them if [the peace offering] was a thanksgiving offering47A thanksgiving offering is one type of peace offering. and the foreleg given the priest from the nazirite's ram, with the bread given with it, and the breast and the leg.48I.e., from the nazirite's ram, the priest is given a foreleg, a hindleg, and the breast. And he is also given a portion of bread that is offered with that sacrifice. Since they are all peace offerings, all of the portions given the priests are considered as one present.
📚 10 + 4 + 10
The Sages count the same 24 gifts differently: 10 in the Temple (splitting sin-offerings and guilt-offerings into separate categories), 4 in Jerusalem (grouping all peace-offering portions as one, adding Temple hides), and 10 in the outlying areas (the regional and universal gifts). This alternate reckoning highlights how the same corpus of law can be organized by different principles — the Rambam's system emphasizes geography of sanctity, while the Sages' system emphasizes practical distribution.

הלכה ט״ז׳
כָּל הַמַּתָּנוֹת שֶׁהֵן תְּלוּיוֹת בַּקָּרְבָּנוֹת תִּתְבָּאֵר כָּל אַחַת וְאַחַת בִּמְקוֹמָהּ בְּהִלְכוֹת קָרְבָּנוֹת. וְכֵן גֵּזֶל הַגֵּר יִתְבָּאֵר בְּהִלְכוֹת גְּזֵלָה. וּכְבָר בֵּאַרְנוּ דִּין שְׂדֵה אֲחֻזָּה וְדִין הַחֲרָמִים בְּהִלְכוֹת עֶרְכִין. וְדִין תְּרוּמָה וּתְרוּמַת מַעֲשֵׂר בְּהִלְכוֹת תְּרוּמוֹת. וּבַהֲלָכוֹת אֵלּוּ אֲבָאֵר מִשְׁפְּטֵי הַמַּתָּנוֹת שֶׁאֵין תְּלוּיִין בְּקָרְבָּנוֹת. וְהֵן: הַבִּכּוּרִים. וְהַחַלָּה. וְהַמַּתָּנוֹת. וְרֵאשִׁית הַגֵּז. וּפִדְיוֹן הַבֵּן. וּפֶטֶר חֲמוֹר:
AIl of the presents that are dependent on the sacrifices will be explained in their appropriate places in the laws of sacrifices.49See the sources given above. Similarly, [the laws pertaining to] the stolen property of a convert will be explained in Hilchot Gezeilah.50See Hilchot Gezeilah 8:5. We have already explained the laws governing an ancestral field and dedication offerings in Hilchot Arachin51Hilchot Arachin, chs. 4-8. and the laws of terumah and terumat ma'aser in Hilchot Terumah. In the present halachot, I will explain the laws of the [priestly] presents that are not dependent on the sacrifices: the first fruits,52See chs. 2-4. challah,53See chs. 5-8. the presents given when slaughtering an animal,54See ch. 9. the first shearings,55See ch. 10. the redemption of a firstborn son,56See ch. 11. the redemption of a firstborn donkey.57See ch. 12.
📖 Roadmap
Sacrifice-dependent gifts are treated in Hilchot Korbanot; this book focuses on the non-sacrifice gifts: first fruits (chs. 2–4), challah (chs. 5–8), slaughtering presents (ch. 9), first shearings (ch. 10), firstborn-son redemption (ch. 11), and firstborn-donkey redemption (ch. 12). Chapter 1 serves as a complete overview — the rest of the book dives into the details of each.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 1
🤝
Covenant of Salt
The 24 priestly gifts are bound by an eternal covenant with Aaron — as permanent and uncorrupting as salt — meaning no generation of priests can renounce or ignore them.
🗺️
Sacred Geography
The degree of holiness of a priestly gift is encoded in where it must be eaten, from the Temple Courtyard inward to anywhere in the world outward.
👨‍👩‍👧
Gendered Access
Most priestly gifts flow through the male priestly line, but five specific presents are distributed equally to female members of the priestly family by scriptural derivation.
📚
Two Valid Systems
The Rambam and the Sages each enumerate the same 24 gifts by different organizing principles, demonstrating that legal taxonomy reflects interpretive perspective, not contradiction.
