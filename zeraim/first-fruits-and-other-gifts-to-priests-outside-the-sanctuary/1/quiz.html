הלכות ביכורים פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many priestly presents were established through the Torah's covenant with Aaron?
Halacha 1 states there are 24 presents given to the priests, all explicitly mentioned in the Torah, established through a covenant with Aaron described as a 'covenant of salt.'
Question 2
What blessing does a priest recite before partaking of a sanctified priestly gift?
Halacha 2 specifies the blessing formula: '...who sanctified us with the sanctity of Aaron and commanded us to partake of...' followed by the name of the specific gift being eaten.
Question 3
Which of the following priestly gifts may be given to FEMALE members of the priestly family?
Halacha 11 teaches that first shearings may be given to female priests, derived scripturally: just as 'the first of the grain' (terumah) is given to females, so too 'the first shearings.' The other three options are Temple gifts restricted to male priests.
Question 4
According to the Sages' alternate reckoning in Halacha 12, how many priestly presents are acquired in the outlying areas outside Jerusalem?
Halacha 12 states the Sages count 10 in the Temple, 4 in Jerusalem, and 10 in the outlying areas (listed in Halacha 15: terumah, terumat ma'aser, challah, first shearings, slaughtering presents, firstborn-son redemption, firstborn-donkey redemption, dedication offerings, ancestral field, and convert's stolen property).
Question 5
Which priestly gift is uniquely acquired from Temple property itself, rather than given by individual Israelites?
Halacha 8 explains that the hides of burnt-offerings (and other most-holy sacrifices) come from the Temple's own property — unlike all other gifts which are given by individual Israelites. The Radbaz notes this is why the Rambam places it in its own category.
