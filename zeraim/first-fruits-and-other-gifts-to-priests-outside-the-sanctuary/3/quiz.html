הלכות ביכורים פרק ג · 5 Questions
Question 1
What punishment does a non-priest incur for eating first fruits after they have entered Jerusalem's walls?
Since first fruits are called terumah, a non-priest who eats them after they enter Jerusalem faces death at the hand of heaven — the same penalty as eating terumah — as stated in Halachah 1.
Question 2
A priest who eats first fruits in Jerusalem before they are placed in the Temple courtyard is liable for:
Halachah 3 teaches that a priest who eats first fruits before courtyard placement — or outside Jerusalem after they entered the city — receives Scriptural lashes, as derived from Deuteronomy 12:17 and 26:10.
Question 3
Why did the Sages enact that the bikkurim declaration be read aloud for all bringers, even those who know how to read?
Halachah 11 explains that those who could not read were staying home out of shame. The Sages therefore ruled that everyone — literate or not — repeats the passage after a reader, so no one would be embarrassed.
Question 4
Which Psalm did the Levites begin singing when the first fruits arrived in the Temple courtyard?
Halachah 13 states that the Levites would begin with Psalm 30:2 — 'I will exalt You God, for You have drawn me up' — a psalm of thanksgiving that matches the themes of the first fruits ceremony.
Question 5
According to the Rambam, how many distinct requirements (factors) must be fulfilled when bringing first fruits?
Halachah 14 enumerates seven requirements: (1) brought to Jerusalem, (2) in a container, (3) declaration recited, (4) accompanied by a peace offering, (5) with song, (6) raised in all directions, and (7) overnight stay in Jerusalem.
