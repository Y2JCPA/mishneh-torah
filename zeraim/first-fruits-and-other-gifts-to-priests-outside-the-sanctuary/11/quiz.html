🎓 Quiz

הלכות ביכורים פרק י״א · 5 Questions
Question 1
If a father fails to redeem his firstborn son, who bears the obligation once the son turns thirteen?
Halachah 2 teaches that if the father did not redeem his son, upon reaching the age of thirteen the son becomes obligated to redeem himself.
Question 2
A father must choose between redeeming his firstborn son and funding a festival pilgrimage to Jerusalem. What does the law require?
Halachah 4 rules that redeeming his son takes precedence over the pilgrimage, deriving this from the juxtaposition of the two commands in Exodus 34:20.
Question 3
A father gives the priest five selaim for his son's redemption but secretly intends for the priest to return the money. The priest does return it. What is the legal status of the son?
Halachah 8 rules that if the priest is given the money with the intent that he return it and he does so, the son is not redeemed — the gift was never genuine. The priest may only return the money after receiving it unconditionally.
Question 4
Which of the following items may NOT be used to pay the five selaim redemption?
Halachah 6 rules that only silver or movable property of inherent monetary value qualifies. Promissory notes — including modern fiat currency — have no inherent worth and cannot redeem the firstborn.
Question 5
Two wives of the same man, both previously childless, give birth to two sons whose identities become mixed. One son dies within thirty days. If both payments were given to a single priest, what happens?
Halachah 21 rules that if both payments went to one priest and one son dies within thirty days, the priest must return five selaim — since no obligation exists for a son who dies before reaching thirty days of age.
