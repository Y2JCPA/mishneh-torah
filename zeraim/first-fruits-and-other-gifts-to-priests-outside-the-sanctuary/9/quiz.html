לרפואת פייגא בת יטא רבקה

🎓 Quiz

הלכות ביכורים פרק ט · 5 Questions
Question 1
Which three body parts must be given as presents to the priest when slaughtering a kosher domesticated animal?
Deuteronomy 18:3 specifies the foreleg (right), jaw, and maw as the three priestly presents — universally known as 'the presents' (H1).
Question 2
Which consecrated animal that was redeemed remains obligated in the priestly presents?
Only an animal with a permanent blemish at the time of consecration retains the obligation after redemption, because its body was never truly sanctified. Animals consecrated when unblemished — even if later blemished — are considered pesulei hamukdashim and are exempt (H2).
Question 3
A priest has established a permanent butcher shop and keeps the priestly presents for himself. What is the ruling?
A priest who established a butcher shop receives no grace period — the presents are expropriated immediately. If he refuses, a ban of ostracism is imposed. The two-or-three-week grace applies only to a priest who slaughters occasionally for the market, not one who runs a shop (H9).
Question 4
An Israelite transgresses and eats the priestly presents without permission. What is his legal obligation?
Because there is no identified plaintiff — the owner may give the presents to any priest he wishes, so no single priest can claim they are owed to him — the transgressor has no legal obligation of restitution, though a moral obligation may exist (H14).
Question 5
According to the tradition derived from Numbers 18:8, in what manner should priests ideally eat the priestly presents?
Numbers 18:8 describes the priestly gifts as given 'for distinction.' The Talmud (Chulin 132b) derives from this that they should be eaten roasted with mustard — in a manner befitting a king — though this is custom and not an absolute obligation (H22).
