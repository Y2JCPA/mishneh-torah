הלכות מתנות עניים פרק י · 5 Questions
Question 1
According to Rambam, what is the highest level of charity?
Halachah 7 teaches that the highest level is supporting someone so that they never need to ask for alms at all — preserving independence and self-esteem.
Question 2
What spiritual transgression is withholding charity compared to?
Halachah 3 draws a direct parallel: just as the Torah calls idol worshippers 'rebellious,' so too is one who turns away from charity called 'rebellious' (using the same term).
Question 3
What was special about the 'secret chamber' in the Temple?
Halachah 8 describes this as the ideal of charity 'for its sake alone' — since neither party knows the other, there can be no ulterior motive on either side.
Question 4
What does Rambam say a distinguished sage who falls into poverty should do?
Halachah 18 states it is preferable to skin animal carcasses than to declare 'I am a great sage, support me' — self-reliance through honest labor is a core value.
Question 5
What fate awaits someone who deceives others into giving him charity he does not need?
Halachah 19 teaches that the deceiver who takes unneeded charity is cursed (Jeremiah 17:5) and will eventually genuinely need the support he falsely claimed.
