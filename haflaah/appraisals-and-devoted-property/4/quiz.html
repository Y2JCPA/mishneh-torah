הלכות ערכים וחרמין פרק ד · 5 Questions
Question 1
What is the Torah's valuation of an ancestral field for a full 50-year Jubilee cycle?
Halachah 2: For the full 50-year cycle, an ancestral field is valued at 50 silver shekalim per chomer (the area fit to sow a chomer of barley).
Question 2
How many days must the sale of consecrated land be announced before it can be sold?
Halachah 27: Consecrated land must be announced morning and evening for 60 consecutive days to attract maximum buyers before being sold.
Question 3
An ancestral field reaches the Jubilee year without being redeemed. What happens?
Halachah 19: A field not redeemed by Jubilee is given to the priests of the priestly watch serving that week in the Temple — distributed equally among all priests of that watch.
Question 4
If a person consecrates his ancestral field the year before Jubilee, can it be redeemed at a reduced rate?
Halachah 9: Consecrating a field fewer than two years before Jubilee means it cannot be redeemed at the Torah's reduced formula rate — the field is consecrated but the reduced rate does not apply.
Question 5
Trees on a consecrated ancestral field — are they automatically included in the consecration?
Halachah 15: When a person consecrates property, he does so with a generous spirit. Trees are automatically consecrated along with the field even without explicit mention.
