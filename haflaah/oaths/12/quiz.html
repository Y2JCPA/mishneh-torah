הלכות שבועות פרק י״ב · 5 Questions
Question 1
After receiving lashes for a false oath, is the person fully atoned?
The false oath is in a special category where death is required for full atonement — lashes and even sincere repentance do not completely remove it.
Question 2
Does swearing 'by the sun' constitute a halachic oath?
Oaths by heaven, earth, sun, or similar are not halachic oaths — only God's name or attributes create binding oath obligations.
Question 3
A Torah scholar swears 'by the Torah.' This means:
For a Torah scholar, 'by the Torah' is understood as referring to the Divine Author — the oath has the weight of swearing by God.
Question 4
Someone hears a colleague recite a blessing unnecessarily. The listener should:
Hearing an unnecessary blessing (a blessing in vain) obligates the listener to respond with grief over the desecration of God's name.
Question 5
The prohibition against mentioning God's name in vain applies to:
The prohibition extends to all divine names and attributes across all languages — not limited to Hebrew or the primary four-letter name.
