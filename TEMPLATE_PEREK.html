God's Existence

הלכות א׳–ג׳ · The Foundation of Foundations
יְסוֹד הַיְסוֹדוֹת וְעַמּוּד הַחׇכְמוֹת — לֵידַע שֶׁיֵּשׁ שָׁם מָצוּי רִאשׁוֹן
ה׳א
There is a Primary Being who brought into existence everything that exists. All things in heaven and earth exist only through His true existence.
ה׳ב
If one would imagine that He does not exist — no other being could possibly exist.
ה׳ג
If one would imagine that nothing else existed — He alone would continue to exist. He does not need any of them, but they all need Him.
🔑 The Logic
The Rambam builds a philosophical proof in 3 steps: God exists → Without Him nothing can exist → Without everything else, He still exists. This proves God is the only necessary being.

True Existence & The Mitzvah

הלכות ד׳–ו׳ · What makes God's existence unique

🟢 God's Existence

  • Necessary — cannot not exist
  • Not dependent on anything
  • "True existence" (אמיתת הימצאו)
  • Infinite, unbounded power

🔴 Everything Else

  • Contingent — could not exist
  • Depends on God
  • "Borrowed" existence
  • Limited, finite
Jeremiah 10:10
וַה׳ אֱלֹקִים אֱמֶת
"And God, your Lord, is true"
He alone is true — nothing else possesses truth like His truth
⚡ Halacha 6 — The Mitzvah
Knowing this is a positive commandment (Exodus 20:2: "I am Hashem your God"). Anyone who entertains the idea that another god exists violates "You shall have no other gods."

God is One

הלכה ז׳ · A unity unlike any other
ה׳ זֶה — אֶחָד הוּא, וְאֵינוֹ שְׁנַיִם וְלֹא יֶתֶר עַל שְׁנַיִם, אֶלָּא אֶחָד
❌

Not "one" like a species

Which includes many individuals

❌

Not "one" like a body

Which can be divided into parts

❌

Not "one" like a simple number

Which can be divided infinitely

✅

A unique unity

That has no parallel in the world

💡 Why This Matters
The Rambam is saying: when we say "God is one," we don't mean what we normally mean by "one." Every other "one" thing is composed of parts, or is one instance of a type. God's oneness is absolutely unique — there's nothing to compare it to.

No Body, No Form

הלכות ח׳–ט׳ · Physical descriptions in Torah
🤔
Question: The Torah says "below His feet," "written by the finger of God," "the hand of God," "the eyes of God," "the ears of God." Does God have a body?
רמ
Rambam: Absolutely not. All of these are adapted to the understanding of humans — "the Torah speaks in the language of man." These are metaphors.
📖
Torah Says
"Hand of God"
🔄
Meaning
God's power
📖
Torah Says
"Eyes of God"
🔄
Meaning
God's awareness
📜 Deuteronomy 4:15
"You did not see any form" — on the day God spoke at Sinai, there was no form. This proves He has no body. If He did, He'd be limited and finite like all physical things.

Moshe's Request

הלכות י׳–י״ב · What can we know about God?
משה
"Please show me Your glory" (Exodus 33:18)

Moshe wanted to know God's true nature — to understand Him as clearly as recognizing a friend's face in a crowd.
ה׳
"You cannot see My face, for no man can see Me and live" (Exodus 33:20)

A living human in a physical body cannot fully grasp God's essence.
רמ
But God did reveal to Moshe what no one before or after attained — he understood God's nature like seeing someone from behind: you know it's them, but you can't see their face.

What Moshe Learned

  • God's attributes of action
  • How God relates to creation
  • More than any prophet ever knew

What Remains Hidden

  • God's essential nature
  • What He truly "is"
  • Beyond any human comprehension

🎓 Key Principles

The four pillars of Chapter 1
🏗️
יסוד היסודות
God's existence is the foundation of everything. Without it, nothing — no Torah, no world, no meaning — can exist. This is why the Rambam starts here.
⚡
Necessary vs. Contingent
Only God's existence is necessary. Everything else is contingent — it exists because God wills it. Remove God, remove everything.
☝️
Absolute Unity
God's oneness is unique — no parts, no body, no division. Not "one" like anything else in creation.
📜
דברה תורה כלשון בני אדם
The Torah speaks in human language. All physical descriptions of God are metaphors — adapted for human understanding.
