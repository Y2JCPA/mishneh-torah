🎓 Quiz

הלכות כלים פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
How many types of vessels are susceptible to impurity according to Scriptural law?
Halacha 1 lists seven: clothes, sackcloth, leather, bone, metal, wood, and earthenware — each derived from Biblical verses.
Question 2
Are vessels made from bones of sea creatures susceptible to impurity?
Halacha 2 teaches that everything from the sea is pure and not susceptible to any form of impurity.
Question 3
By what authority are glass containers susceptible to impurity?
Halacha 4: the Sages decreed impurity for glass containers since they are made from sand like earthenware.
Question 4
Which materials produce vessels that are ALWAYS pure — susceptible to no form of impurity?
Halacha 5: vessels from animal dung, stone, or earth are always pure — neither Scripturally nor Rabbinically.
Question 5
Flat wooden items used as a support for a zav — under which law are they susceptible?
Halacha 9: flat wooden/leather/bone items serving as a zav-support are susceptible by Scriptural law (Lev. 15:4).
