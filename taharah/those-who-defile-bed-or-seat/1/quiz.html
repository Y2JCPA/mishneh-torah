הלכות מטמאי משכב ומושב פרק א · 5 Questions
Question 1
From what age can a female infant contract nidah impurity?
Halacha 3 states: 'An infant girl one day old can contract nidah impurity. At ten days old, she can contract zivah impurity.'
Question 2
What is the status of blood released during a Cesarean section?
Halacha 8 rules that uterine blood released together with the child during a Cesarean section is a primary source of impurity like nidah blood.
Question 3
Which three fluids of a zav are primary sources of impurity according to Scriptural Law?
Halacha 13 explicitly lists the zav's saliva, semen, and urine as the three primary sources of impurity from among his nine bodily fluids.
Question 4
How does impurity law treat a person who is born sexually impotent (a saris)?
Halacha 4 states that a person who was castrated or born sexually impotent may contract impurity because of a zav discharge like other men.
Question 5
If a single white drop is discharged by a woman, what is her status?
Halacha 10 rules: 'If a woman discharged one drop of a white fluid, she remains pure, for that certainly comes from outside the uterus.'
