Dew and Rain on Produce: Intent Determines Susceptibility

הלכות א׳–ד׳
הלכה א׳
פֵּרוֹת שֶׁיָּרַד הַדֶּלף לתוֹכָן וְעֵרְבָן כְּדֵי שֶׁיִּתְנַגְּבוּ לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ:
When water from a drainpipe descends into produce and the owner mixed the produce so that it would dry, the produce does not become susceptible to impurity.
הלכה ב׳
הַמַּעֲלֶה פֵּרוֹתָיו לַגַּג מִפְּנֵי הַכְּנִימָה וְיָרַד עֲלֵיהֶן הַטַּל לֹא הֻכְשַׁר. וְאִם נִתְכַּוֵּן שֶׁיֵּרֵד עֲלֵיהֶן הַטַּל הֻכְשְׁרוּ. לְפִיכָךְ אִם הֶעֱלוּם חֵרֵשׁ שׁוֹטֶה וְקָטָן אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁחִשְּׁבוּ שֶׁיֵּרֵד הַטַּל עֲלֵיהֶן לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ שֶׁיֵּשׁ לָהֶן מַעֲשֵׂה דִּין תּוֹרָה וְאֵין לָהֶן מַחֲשָׁבָה אֲפִלּוּ מִדִּבְרֵי סוֹפְרִים. הָפְכוּ בָּהֶן קְטַנִּים עַל הַגַּג הֲרֵי אֵלּוּ מֻכְשָׁרִין שֶׁאִם הָיְתָה מַחֲשָׁבָה שֶׁל קָטָן נִכֶּרֶת מִתּוֹךְ מַעֲשָׂיו מַחְשַׁבְתּוֹ מוֹעֶלֶת מִדִּבְרֵיהֶן:
When a person brings his produce to the roof so that it does not become worm-infested and dew descends upon it, it does not become susceptible to impurity. If he intended that the dew descend upon it, it becomes susceptible to impurity. Therefore, if a deafmute, a intellectually or emotionally compromised individual, or a minor brought the produce up to the roof, even if they had the intent that dew descend upon it, it does not become susceptible to impurity. The rationale is that their deeds are significant according to Scriptural Law, but their intent is not significant, even according to Rabbinic Law.
If minors turned the produce over on the roof, it becomes susceptible to impurity. For if the intent of a minor is obvious from his actions, his intent has an effect according to Rabbinic Law.
הלכה ג׳
הַמַּעֲלֶה אֶת הָאֲגֻדּוֹת וְאֶת הַקְּצִיעוֹת וְאֶת הַשּׁוּם לַגַּג בִּשְׁבִיל שֶׁיַּמְתִּינוּ וְיֵרֵד עֲלֵיהֶן הַטַּל לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ. וְאֵין אוֹמְרִין הוֹאִיל וְהַכּל יוֹדְעִין שֶׁהַטַּל יוֹרֵד הֲרֵי זֶה בִּרְצוֹנוֹ שֶׁהֲרֵי לֹא הֶעֱלָם אֶלָּא כְּדֵי שֶׁיַּמְתִּינוּ:
When a person brings bundles of vegetables, dried figs, or garlic to a roof so that they will be preserved and dew descended upon them, they do not become susceptible to ritual impurity. We do not say that since everyone knows that dew will descend, it is as if he willfully exposed them to water. The rationale is that he brought them up to the roof solely so that they should be preserved.
הלכה ד׳
הַמּוֹלִיךְ חִטִּין לִטְחֹן וְיָרְדוּ עֲלֵיהֶן גְּשָׁמִים אִם שָׂמַח הֻכְשְׁרוּ. הָיוּ זֵיתָיו נְתוּנִין בַּגַּג וְיָרְדוּ עֲלֵיהֶן גְּשָׁמִים אִם שָׂמַח הֻכְשְׁרוּ. הַחַמָּרִים שֶׁהָיוּ עוֹבְרִים בַּנָּהָר וְנָפְלוּ שַׂקֵּיהֶן בַּמַּיִם וְהֶעֱלוּם אִם שָׂמְחוּ הֻכְשְׁרוּ הַפֵּרוֹת וְהַמַּיִם שֶׁעַל הַשַּׂקִּין הִנָּם תְּלוּשִׁין בְּרָצוֹן שֶׁהֲרֵי שָׂמְחוּ:
When a person brings wheat to be ground into flour and rain falls upon it, if he was happy, it becomes susceptible to impurity. When his olives had been placed on the roof and rain descended, if he was happy, they were made susceptible to impurity. When donkey-drivers were crossing a river and their sacks fell into the water, and they lifted them up, if they were happy that the produce became wet, the produce was made susceptible to impurity. The water that is on the sacks is considered as having been uprooted willfully, for they were happy that the sacks became wet.
🌦️ Dew and Rain
When water from a drainpipe descends on produce and the owner mixes it to dry, the produce does not become susceptible. Produce brought to a roof to avoid worm-infestation — if dew descends, it is not susceptible unless the person intended for the dew. Bundles of vegetables or dried figs on a roof — even though everyone knows dew will descend, it does not make them susceptible unless specifically intended. Happiness after rain falls on olives or wheat makes them susceptible.
4/8

Sacks, Jugs, and Produce Absorbing Water

הלכות ה׳–ו׳
הלכה ה׳
שַׂק שֶׁהוּא מָלֵא זֵרְעוֹנִים וְנָתוּן עַל גַּבֵּי הַנָּהָר אוֹ עַל פִּי הַבּוֹר אוֹ עַל מַעֲלוֹת הַמְּעָרָה וְשָׁאֲבוּ הֻכְשְׁרוּ:
When a sack that was filled with seeds and was placed on the edge of a river, at the opening to a cistern, or on the steps leading to a storage cavern of water, if they absorbed water, they become susceptible to impurity.
הלכה ו׳
חָבִית שֶׁהִיא מְלֵאָה פֵּרוֹת שֶׁנְּתָנָהּ בְּתוֹךְ הַמַּשְׁקִין אוֹ מְלֵאָה מַשְׁקִין שֶׁנְּתָנָהּ בְּתוֹךְ הַפֵּרוֹת וְשָׁאֲבוּ הֻכְשְׁרוּ. בְּאֵלּוּ מַשְׁקִין אָמְרוּ בְּמַיִם וּבְיַיִן וּבְחֹמֶץ אֲבָל שְׁאָר הַמַּשְׁקִין אֵין מְשָׁאֲבִין מִן הַחֶרֶס כְּדֵי שֶׁיַּכְשִׁירוּ הַפֵּרוֹת שֶׁבְּצִדָּן:
When an earthenware jug containing produce was placed in liquids or a jug containing liquids was placed among produce and the produce absorbed the liquids, it becomes susceptible to impurity.
With regard to which liquids was the above said: With regard to water, wine, and vinegar. Other liquids are not absorbed through the walls of earthenware containers causing produce to be made susceptible to ritual impurity.
🫙 Absorption
A sack of seeds placed near a river, cistern opening, or storage cavern steps becomes susceptible when the seeds absorb water. An earthenware jug of produce placed in liquids, or a jug of liquids placed among produce — the absorbed liquid makes the produce susceptible. The rule applies only to liquids that make food susceptible — the seven designated liquids.
5/8

Warm Bread, Sprinkling Water, and Moist Sand

הלכות ז׳–ח׳
הלכה ז׳
הָרוֹדֶה פַּת חַמָּה וּנְתָנָהּ עַל פִּי חָבִית שֶׁל יַיִן אִם הָיְתָה פַּת חִטִּין לֹא הֻכְשְׁרָה. וְאִם הָיְתָה שֶׁל שְׂעוֹרִים הֻכְשְׁרָה מִפְּנֵי שֶׁהַשְּׂעוֹרִים שׁוֹאֲבוֹת. וְכֵן אִם הָיָה הַיַּיִן טָמֵא וְהָיְתָה פַּת חִטִּים טְהוֹרָה וְאִם הָיְתָה שְׂעוֹרִים נִטְמְאָה מִפְּנֵי שֶׁשָּׁאֲבָה מַשְׁקִין טְמֵאִין:
The following rules apply when a person removes a hot loaf of bread from an oven and places it on the opening of a jug of wine: If the loaf was from wheat flour, it does not become susceptible to impurity. If it was from barley flour, it does become susceptible to impurity, because barley is absorbent. Similarly, if the wine was impure, if the loaf was from wheat, it is pure. If it was from barley, it becomes impure, because it absorbed impure liquids.
הלכה ח׳
הַמְרַבֵּץ אֶת בֵּיתוֹ וְנָתַן בּוֹ חִטִּים וְטָנְנוּ. אִם מֵחֲמַת הַמַּיִם הֻכְשְׁרוּ. וְאִם מֵחֲמַת הַסֶּלַע לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ. הַמְכַבֵּס אֶת כְּסוּתוֹ בַּעֲרֵבָה וְנָתַן בָּהּ חִטִּים וְטָנְנוּ אִם מֵחֲמַת הַמַּיִם הֻכְשְׁרוּ וְאִם מֵחֲמַת הָעֲרֵבָה לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ:
When a person sprinkles water on the floor of his house and then placed wheat upon it and it became moist, if it became moist because of the water, it becomes susceptible to impurity. If it became moist because of the stone, it does not become susceptible to impurity.
When a person washes his garment in a kneading trough and afterwards he placed wheat in it and it became moist, if it became moist because of the water, it becomes susceptible to impurity. If it became moist because of the kneading trough, it does not become susceptible to impurity.
🍞 Warm Bread
A hot wheat loaf placed on a wine jug does not become susceptible — wheat flour resists absorption. Barley flour bread placed on a wine jug does become susceptible. When a person sprinkles water on the floor and places wheat on it: if the wheat became moist from the water, it is susceptible; if from the stone (natural floor moisture), it is not.
6/8

Threshing Floors, Grasses, Breath, Biting, and Changed Intent

הלכות ט׳–י״ז
הלכה ט׳
הַטּוֹמֵן פֵּרוֹת בַּחוֹל כְּדֵי שֶׁיַּרְטִיבוּ הֻכְשְׁרוּ. הַטּוֹמֵן בַּטִּיט הַנָּגוּב אִם יֵשׁ בּוֹ מַשְׁקֶה טוֹפֵחַ הֻכְשְׁרוּ וְאִם לָאו לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ:
When a person buries his produce in sand so that it will become moist, it becomes subject to impurity. When he buries it in dry mud, if the mud is moist enough that a hand that touches it will become wet, the produce becomes subject to impurity. If not, it does not.
הלכה י׳
הַמְרַבֵּץ אֶת גָּרְנוֹ אֵינוֹ חוֹשֵׁשׁ שֶׁמָּא יֵכְשְׁרוּ חִטָּיו אִם טָנְנוּ בּוֹ וְאַף עַל פִּי שֶׁהוּא שָׂמֵחַ:
When a person sprinkles water on his threshing floor, he need not worry that his wheat became susceptible to impurity if the kernels came in contact with water even though he is satisfied that this happened.
הלכה י״א׳
הַמְלַקֵּט עֲשָׂבִים כְּשֶׁהַטַּל יוֹרֵד עֲלֵיהֶן לְחַפּוֹת בָּהֶן הַחִטִּין לֹא הֻכְשְׁרוּ וְאִם נִתְכַּוִּן לְכָךְ הֻכְשְׁרוּ:
When a person gathers grasses upon which dew had descended to use for covering wheat, the wheat does not become susceptible to impurity. If this was his intent, it does become susceptible.
הלכה י״ב׳
הַמְרַבֵּץ אֶת בֵּיתוֹ בְּמַיִם טְמֵאִים וְנָתַן בּוֹ שִׁבֳּלִים וְטָנְנוּ אִם יֵשׁ עֲלֵיהֶן מַשְׁקֶה טוֹפֵחַ הֲרֵי אֵלּוּ טְמֵאִין וְאִם לָאו טְהוֹרִין:
When a person sprinkles impure water on the floor of his house and then placed stalks of grain there and they become moist, they become impure if there is enough moisture upon them that the hand of one who touches them also becomes moist. If not, they are pure.
הלכה י״ג׳
הַנּוֹעֵר אֲגֻדָּה שֶׁל יָרָק שֶׁהָיוּ עָלֶיהָ מַשְׁקִין וְיָרְדוּ מִצַּד הָעֶלְיוֹן לַצַּד הַתַּחְתּוֹן לֹא הֻכְשַׁר:
When a person shakes a bunch of vegetables that had liquid on them and the liquid descended from the upper ones to the lower ones, they do not become susceptible to impurity.
הלכה י״ד׳
הַמַּעֲלֶה שַׂקִּין מְלֵאִין פֵּרוֹת מִן הַנָּהָר וּנְתָנָן זֶה עַל גַּבֵּי זֶה הֻכְשַׁר הַתַּחְתּוֹן בַּמַּיִם שֶׁיָּרְדוּ לוֹ מִן הָעֶלְיוֹן שֶׁהֲרֵי בִּרְצוֹנוֹ הִנִּיחַ זֶה עַל גַּבֵּי זֶה:
When a person lifts sacks full of produce from the river and places them one on top of the other, the lower one becomes susceptible to impurity because of the water descending from the upper one, for he willfully placed one on top of the other.
הלכה ט״ו׳
הַנּוֹפֵחַ בַּעֲדָשִׁים לְבָדְקָן אִם הֵן יָפוֹת וְהִזִּיעוּ הֻכְשְׁרוּ בְּהֶבֶל פִּיו מִפְּנֵי שֶׁהוּא מִתּוֹלְדוֹת הַמַּיִם. וְכֵן הָאוֹכֵל שֻׁמְשְׁמִין בָּאֶצְבַּע הֻכְשְׁרוּ בְּמַשְׁקֵה פִּיו וְשֶׁעַל אֶצְבָּעוֹ:
When a person blows on lentils to see if they are good for the purpose of cooking, if water collects on them, they become susceptible to impurity because of the moisture in his breath which is a derivative of water. Similarly, when a person eats sesame seeds with his finger, they become susceptible to impurity because of the moisture in his breath and on his finger.
הלכה ט״ז׳
נָשַׁךְ בָּאֹכֶל וְנָפַל הָאֹכֶל הֲרֵי הַמַּשְׁקֶה שֶׁעַל הָאֹכֶל שֶׁלֹּא לְרָצוֹן. הָיָה אוֹכֵל זֵיתִים פְּצוּעִים וּתְמָרִים רְטֻבּוֹת וְכָל שֶׁהוּא רוֹצֶה לָמֹץ אֶת גַּרְעִינָתוֹ וְנָפַל מִפִּיו הֲרֵי הַמַּשְׁקֶה שֶׁעָלָיו בְּרָצוֹן. הָיָה אוֹכֵל זֵיתִים נְגוּבִין וּתְמָרִים יְבֵשׁוֹת וְכָל שֶׁאֵינוֹ רוֹצֶה לָמֹץ אֶת גַּרְעִינָתוֹ וְנָפַל מִפִּיו הֲרֵי הַמַּשְׁקֶה שֶׁעָלָיו שֶׁלֹּא בְּרָצוֹן:
When a person bites food and then it falls from his hand, the liquid on the food is not considered as having been placed there willfully. If a person was eating olives that had been broken open, moist dates, or any other produce whose pit he desired to suck, but it fell from his mouth, the liquid on it is considered as having been placed there willfully. If he was eating dry olives, dried dates, any other produce whose pit he does not desire to suck, and it fell from his mouth, the liquid on it is not considered as having been placed there willfully.
הלכה י״ז׳
עֻלְשִׁין שֶׁלִּקְּטָן לִבְהֵמָה וֶהֱדִיחָן וְחִשֵּׁב עֲלֵיהֶן לְאָדָם אַחַר כָּךְ כְּבָר בֵּאַרְנוּ שֶׁהֵן צְרִיכִים הֶכְשֵׁר שֵׁנִי. וְאִם הָיָה עֲלֵיהֶן מַשְׁקֶה טוֹפֵחַ כְּשֶׁחִשֵּׁב עֲלֵיהֶן לְאָדָם הֲרֵי אֵלּוּ מֻכְשָׁרִין:
We have already explained that when a person gathered endives for an animal and washed them, but changed his mind afterwards and thought to use them for human consumption, they need to be exposed to water a second time to make foods susceptible to impurity. If, however, there was tangible moisture on them when he thought to use them for human consumption, they are susceptible to impurity.
💨 Breath and Touch
Sprinkling water on a threshing floor — the owner need not worry his wheat became susceptible, even though he is satisfied. Gathering dew-covered grasses to cover wheat — the wheat does not become susceptible unless that was the intent. Blowing on lentils to check their quality creates moisture from breath — a derivative of water — making them susceptible. A person eating olives: if he desired the liquid from the pit, the liquid on removed food is considered desired.

🎓 Key Principles

Chapter 14
🌿
Common Knowledge Does Not Equal Intent
Even though everyone knows dew will fall on roof-stored produce, that knowledge alone does not constitute the required intent to make the produce susceptible.
😊
Retroactive Happiness Creates Susceptibility
If the owner becomes happy after rain or dew falls on his produce, that retroactive satisfaction triggers susceptibility to impurity.
💨
Breath Is a Derivative of Water
The moisture in a person's breath is classified as a derivative of water, and products moistened by intentional blowing become susceptible to impurity.
🪨
Source of Moisture Matters
Whether moisture on produce came from the owner's water or from the natural stone floor determines whether the produce becomes susceptible.
