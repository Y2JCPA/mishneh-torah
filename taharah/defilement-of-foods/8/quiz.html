הלכות טומאת אוכלין פרק ח · 5 Questions
Question 1
When are loaves baking in an oven considered joined so that a tevel yom touching one disqualifies all?
Halacha 1 states that loaves attached to each other in an oven are considered joined until the surface of the bread hardens. Once hardened, they are considered separate.
Question 2
A tevel yom touches a stirred egg placed on terumah vegetables. What does he disqualify?
Halacha 3 states that if a tevel yom touches the egg, he disqualifies only the stalk of the vegetable below the place he touched. If the egg bubbled like a helmet, it disqualifies the whole vegetable.
Question 3
A perforated barrel is closed with the hand of a tevel yom. What is the result?
Halacha 5 states that if a person who immersed that day closed the hole of a perforated barrel with his hand, the contents of the entire barrel are disqualified.
Question 4
If terumah oil rests on top of ordinary thick stew and a tevel yom touches the oil, what is disqualified?
Halacha 9 states that if oil that is terumah is resting upon a thick stew of ordinary food and a tevel yom touched the oil, he disqualifies only the oil.
Question 5
When thick terumah stew contains ordinary garlic and oil, and a tevel yom touches any part, what is disqualified?
Halacha 11 states that when there is a thick terumah stew with ordinary garlic and oil, and a tevel yom touched part of the stew, the oil, or the garlic, he disqualifies everything.
